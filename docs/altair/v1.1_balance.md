# Altair — Balance Overhaul v1.1

> **Purpose:** Address critical balance failures making the game trivially easy. Players should struggle to reach 20:00 even on Knight. A full clear should feel like an achievement, not a guarantee.
> **Reference Games:** Vampire Survivors (post-1.0 balance), 20 Minutes Till Dawn, Brotato, Soulstone Survivors
> **Philosophy:** The player should feel powerful by minute 10 but genuinely threatened by minute 15. Minute 18+ should feel like controlled panic. Sitting still and winning should be impossible at any point past minute 3.

## 1. Root Cause Analysis

The current balance has five compounding problems that feed into each other:

### Problem 1: Uncapped Pierce Creates Passive Play

Most weapons either inherently pierce infinitely (Storm Bow, Knife Storm, Garlic/Soul Eater, Death Ray chains, Lightning/Thunderstorm) or gain pierce through evolution. Once a player has 2–3 AoE/pierce weapons at mid levels, every enemy dies within frames of spawning. The player can literally stop moving and win.

**Fix:** Introduce pierce limits on all projectile weapons. No weapon should have infinite pierce by default. Pierce becomes a stat that must be invested in. AoE weapons get radius reductions.

### Problem 2: Exponential Player DPS vs. Polynomial Enemy HP

Player damage scales as: `baseDmg × 1.15^(level-1) × might × passives`. This is **exponential**. A level 8 weapon does 2.66× base before Might. With 2.0× Might (easy to reach), that's 5.32× base. With 4 weapons firing, total DPS is astronomical.

Enemy HP scales as: `base × (1 + t×0.12 + t²×0.008)`. At minute 20, this is 6.6×. A Shambler goes from 8 HP to 53 HP. But a single level 8 Broad Sword with 1.5× Might does `20 × 2.66 × 1.5 = 79.8` damage per swing every 1.2s. One weapon one-shots the toughest-scaled Shambler.

**Fix:** Flatten player damage scaling, steepen enemy HP scaling dramatically, reduce Might accessibility.

### Problem 3: XP Curve is Far Too Generous

The current formula `5 + level×3 + level²×0.4` lets players hit level 30+ by minute 20. Combined with XP gem scaling (`1 + t×0.05`), late-game players level every 10–15 seconds. Each level is another weapon upgrade or passive, further accelerating the snowball.

**Fix:** Steeper XP curve (cubic component), remove XP gem time-scaling entirely, cap effective level lower.

### Problem 4: Spawn Budget Doesn't Keep Up

92 threat/second at minute 20 with a 300-enemy cap means screens never feel truly dangerous to a geared player. In Vampire Survivors, the final minutes feature thousands of enemies on screen simultaneously. The budget here is too conservative.

**Fix:** Dramatically increase late-game spawn budgets, raise enemy cap, add "horde surge" mechanics.

### Problem 5: Bosses Are Stat Checks, Not Threats

The Hollow King has 800 HP. By minute 5, a moderately built player deals 80–120 DPS across their weapons. The King dies in 7–10 seconds. Terminus at 8,000 HP faces a player doing 400–800 DPS with evolved weapons. It dies in 10–20 seconds. Bosses need to be genuine survival encounters.

**Fix:** Massive boss HP increases, add damage cap mechanics, make boss fights about survival not DPS racing.

## 2. Weapon Overhaul

### Global Changes

| Change | Old | New | Rationale |
|--------|-----|-----|-----------|
| **Damage per level** | +15% compounding | +10% compounding | Lv8 goes from 2.66× → 1.95× base. Significant DPS reduction across all weapons |
| **Pierce stat (NEW)** | Not a stat | New stat, base 0 for all players | Projectiles hit 1 enemy by default. Pierce adds pass-through targets |
| **Max weapons per run** | 6 | 6 | Unchanged |
| **Max weapon level** | 8 | 8 | Unchanged |
| **Level 2/4/6 bonus** | +1 base effect each | +1 at 2, +1 at 5 only | Fewer free scaling breakpoints |
| **Evolution requirement** | Weapon lv8 + passive (any level) | Weapon lv8 + passive **lv3+** | Demands more investment before payoff |

**New damage formula:**

```
weaponDamage = baseDamage × Math.pow(1.10, level - 1) × might
weaponCooldown = baseCooldown × cdr / attackSpeed
```

**Level 8 damage comparison:**

| | Old (1.15^7) | New (1.10^7) |
|---|---|---|
| Multiplier | 2.66× | 1.95× |
| Broad Sword (base 20) | 53.2 | 39.0 |
| Fire Wand (base 30) | 79.8 | 58.5 |

## 3. Evolved Weapon Nerfs

Evolutions should feel like significant upgrades but not instant win conditions. The current evolutions turn weapons into screen-clearing death machines with no counterplay.

### Global Evolution Changes

- Evolution now requires the passive at **level 3 or higher** (was: any level)
- Evolved weapons inherit the base weapon's current level scaling, they don't get a separate damage bonus on top
- All evolved weapons that had "infinite pierce" or "hits all enemies" now have explicit target caps

| # | Evolved Weapon | Old Behavior | New Behavior |
|---|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Radiant Claymore** | 180° arc, +50% damage, holy shockwave every 3rd swing | 140° arc, +30% damage. Shockwave every 4th swing: 120px radius, deals 50% of swing damage. **Max 10 targets per shockwave** |

## 6. XP & Leveling Curve Rework

This is one of the most impactful changes. Players currently reach level 32–35 by minute 20. The target is **level 22–26** with good play.

### New XP Formula

Old: `xp_required(level) = 5 + level × 3 + level² × 0.4`

New: `xp_required(level) = 8 + level × 4 + level² × 0.6 + level³ × 0.008`

The cubic term makes late-game leveling dramatically slower.

| Level | Old XP Required | New XP Required | Change |
|-------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| 1→2 | 9 | 13 | +44% |
| 2→3 | 16 | 19 | +19% |
| 5→6 | 40 | 49 | +23% |
| 10→11 | 95 | 117 | +23% |
| 15→16 | 170 | 217 | +28% |
| 20→21 | 245 | 357 | +46% |
| 25→26 | 340 | 545 | +60% |
| 30→31 | 455 | 789 | +73% |

### Remove XP Gem Time-Scaling

**Delete the XP scaling formula entirely.** Old: `xp_value(t) = base_xp × (1 + t_minutes × 0.05)`. New: XP gems always give their flat value (1 / 5 / 25). No time-based increase.

This was a hidden accelerant that made late-game leveling too fast. Removing it means the player's XP income is purely based on kill rate and pickup efficiency, not a free time-based multiplier.

### Growth Stat Nerf

Growth now has **diminishing returns** above 1.5×:

```
effective_growth = growth <= 1.5 ? growth : 1.5 + (growth - 1.5) × 0.4
```

| 1.3× | 1.3× | 1.3× |
| 1.5× | 1.5× | 1.5× |
| 2.0× | 2.0× | 1.7× |
| 2.5× | 2.5× | 1.9× |

### Expected Level by Time (New Curve)

The scaling formulas are the most critical change. Enemy HP must outpace player DPS growth in the final 5 minutes to create genuine danger.

## 10. Boss Overhaul

Bosses need to be genuine survival encounters lasting **45–90 seconds** minimum, not 10-second DPS checks.

### Global Boss Changes

1. **Massive HP increases** across the board.
2. **Damage cap per second (NEW):** Bosses have a maximum amount of damage they can receive per second. This prevents hyper-optimized builds from deleting bosses instantly while ensuring all builds can eventually win. The cap scales with time (later bosses have higher caps because the player is expected to be stronger).
3. **Boss HP regeneration:** Bosses passively regenerate a small amount of HP to punish low-DPS builds and create a "DPS check" floor.
4. **Enrage timers:** If a boss is alive for too long, it enrages (increased speed, damage, new attacks). This creates urgency without making the fight impossible.

At 120 DPS cap, the King takes a minimum of ~18 seconds to kill (2200 HP / 120 DPS). Realistically with player DPS at minute 5 being ~60–90 DPS, the fight lasts **30–45 seconds**. The regen (5/s) drains ~150–225 HP over the fight, adding ~10% effective HP for mid-tier builds.

## 13. New Mechanics

### 13.1 Pierce as a Player Stat

**Pierce** is a new stat that determines how many enemies a projectile passes through.

| Stat | Base Value | Soft Cap | Hard Cap |
|------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Pierce | 0 | 5 | 10 |

Sources of Pierce:
- Piercing Lens passive: +1 per level (max +5)
- Quiver passive: +1 at levels 1, 3, 5 (max +3)
- Star Map passive: +1 at levels 2, 5 (max +2)
- Specific weapon evolutions grant bonus pierce to their own projectiles (not global)

Pierce only applies to **projectile-type weapons**: Arcane Bolt, Iron Shortbow, Throwing Daggers, Fire Wand, Temporal Shard, Crimson Whip, Cultist projectiles (enemy). It does not apply to auras, beams, ground AoE, or orbitals (those have their own target caps).

**Weapon-specific pierce bonus (evolution only):**
| Evolved Weapon | Inherent Pierce Bonus |
|---|---|
| Storm Bow | +4 (on top of global pierce) |
| Knife Storm | +0 (bounces instead) |
| Arcane Barrage | +0 (AoE explosion instead) |
| Inferno Staff | +0 (explosion instead) |

### 13.2 Boss Damage Caps

As detailed in §10, bosses have a **maximum damage they can receive per second** from all sources combined. Damage above the cap in any given second is simply discarded. This is invisible to the player (no UI indicator) and prevents boss fights from being trivialized by any build.

Implementation: track total damage to boss per game-tick. If cumulative damage in the last 1.0 seconds exceeds the cap, ignore additional damage until the next 1-second window.

### 13.3 Horde Surges

As detailed in §9. Periodic mass-spawns starting at minute 8, every 90 seconds. Tests the player's AoE and movement.

### 13.4 Enemy Armor (NEW)

Select late-game enemies now have innate Armor (flat damage reduction per hit), just like bosses have always had:

| Enemy | New Armor Value |
|-------|----------------|
| Bone Golem | 2 |
| Death Knight | 3 |
| Vampire Noble | 1 |
| Lich | 2 |

This specifically nerfs rapid-fire low-damage weapons (Throwing Daggers, Garlic) against these enemies while leaving slower high-damage weapons effective. It encourages weapon diversity.

### 13.5 Diminishing Returns on AoE (NEW)

When an AoE effect (weapon or ability) hits more than **12 enemies simultaneously**, damage to enemies beyond the 12th is reduced:

```
effective_damage(n) = n <= 12 ? full_damage : full_damage × (12 / n)
```

| Enemies Hit | Damage per Enemy |
|-------------|-----------------|
| 1–12 | 100% |
| 15 | 80% |
| 20 | 60% |
| 30 | 40% |
| 50 | 24% |

This prevents any single weapon from efficiently clearing 50+ enemies simultaneously. The player needs multiple weapons covering different areas, not one mega-AoE.

## 15. Target Benchmarks

These are the design targets for a player with **moderate meta upgrades** (roughly 50% of shop completed). An experienced player with full upgrades should do better. A new player with zero upgrades should do worse.

### Solo (50% Meta)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average run survival time | **12–15 minutes** |
| Clear rate (survive 20:00) | **15–25%** of runs |
| Full clear with boss kill rate | **10–15%** of runs |
| Average level at death (12 min) | ~14–16 |
| Average level at 20:00 (if survived) | ~23–26 |
| Kills at 20:00 | ~4,000–6,000 |
| Average coins per run (all outcomes) | 120–250 |
| Coins per full clear | 500–750 |

### Solo (0% Meta, First Runs)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average survival | **6–9 minutes** |
| Clear rate | **<1%** |
| Expected coins | 30–80 |

### Solo (100% Meta, Experienced)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average survival | **16–18 minutes** |
| Clear rate | **40–55%** |
| Coins per full clear | 650–1,000 |

### Difficulty Feel by Minute

| Minute | Player Should Feel |
|--------|-------------------|
| 0–3 | Comfortable, learning the map and movement |
| 3–5 | Engaged, first real threats, thinking about builds |
| 5–8 | Powerful but tested. Boss fight is a real encounter |
