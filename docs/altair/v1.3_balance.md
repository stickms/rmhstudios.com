# Altair — Balance Patch v1.3: Meta Store & Evolution Rework

> **Scope:** Complete rework of the weapon evolution system, passive item redesign, and expanded meta store.
> **Core Problems Addressed:**
> 1. Players pick evolution passives purely as stat sticks, not because they're interesting
> 2. Evolution passives overlap with generic stat passives (Gauntlet = Armor, War Paint = Might — why do both exist?)
> 3. Duplicate evolved weapons make no sense (two Radiant Claymores?)
> 4. The meta store is too small and lacks meaningful choice
> **Design Goals:** Every item should feel like a real pickup with its own identity. Evolutions should feel like a reward for a deliberate build path, not an automatic upgrade.

## 1. Evolution System Rework

### Old System Problems

The v2.0/v2.1 system worked like this:
- Weapons level from 1→8 through level-up selections
- At level 8, if the player holds a specific passive item at level 3+, the weapon auto-evolves
- The passive items were stat boosters (e.g., Gauntlet = +1 Armor/level) that also happened to be evolution keys

This created several issues:
- **Passives were boring.** "+5% Might" is not an interesting pickup. Players grabbed them solely for evolution access.
- **Stat overlap.** Gauntlet (+Armor) does the same thing as the meta shop Armor upgrade. War Paint (+Might) does the same thing as meta shop Might. There's no reason for both to exist.
- **No decision weight.** Since passives were just stat sticks, there was never a reason NOT to take the evolution passive for your weapon. The "choice" was predetermined.
- **Duplicate evolutions.** Nothing stopped two players from evolving the same weapon, and nothing stopped a single player from... well, the system just didn't address uniqueness at all.

### New System: Evolution Catalysts

**Evolution catalysts** are a new item category, separate from generic passives. They are unique items with **active or mechanically interesting effects** that also happen to enable weapon evolution.

**Key rules:**

1. **Catalysts are not stat sticks.** They don't give flat "+X% to stat." They have unique gameplay effects — procs, conditionals, on-hit triggers, or build-around mechanics.
2. **Catalysts have 3 levels** (not 5). They're cheaper to max but you get them for the effect, not for grinding stats.
3. **One evolution per weapon, once, ever.** When a weapon reaches level 8 and the player holds the matching catalyst at level 3, the weapon evolves. The catalyst is **consumed** in the process. The catalyst disappears from your passive slots, freeing up space. This is the tradeoff: you lose a useful passive effect to gain a powerful evolved weapon.
4. **No duplicate evolved weapons.** If a weapon has already been evolved (by you or, in multiplayer, if you already hold that evolved weapon), it cannot be evolved again. You cannot hold two Radiant Claymores.
5. **Catalysts are useful even without evolving.** If you pick up a catalyst but don't have the matching weapon, or your weapon isn't level 8 yet, the catalyst still provides its unique effect. It's never a dead pickup.

### Evolution Flow

```
WEAPON reaches Level 8
    + CATALYST at Level 3 in passive slots
    → EVOLUTION triggers automatically
    → Weapon transforms into Evolved form
    → Catalyst is CONSUMED (removed from passive slots)
    → Passive slot is freed (5/6 passives → can pick up something new)
```

This creates a satisfying arc: early game you pick up the catalyst for its effect, mid game you level both weapon and catalyst, late game the evolution fires and you get a power spike PLUS a free passive slot. It rewards planning.

## 2. Evolution Catalysts

Each catalyst has a unique mechanical identity. None of them give flat stat bonuses. All of them are interesting pickups even if you never evolve.

**Catalysts have 3 levels.** Level-up selections can offer catalyst upgrades just like weapon/passive upgrades.

### Catalyst 1: Warden's Crest
**Evolves:** Broad Sword → Radiant Claymore

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | After taking damage, gain a **damage shield** equal to **8% of damage taken** for 3 seconds. Shield absorbs further hits. (Minimum shield: 3 HP) |
| 2 | Shield amount increased to **12%**. Shield duration: 4 seconds. |
| 3 | Shield amount: **16%**. While shield is active, your next melee attack deals **+20% damage**. **Evolution ready.** |

**Identity:** Reactive defense. You take a hit, you get a small shield, and if you counter-attack while shielded you hit harder. Encourages aggressive melee play. Useful on any melee-oriented character, not just Knight.

## 3. Generic Passive Items

With catalysts handling all evolution duties, **generic passives are now pure utility/stat items**. They don't evolve anything. Their job is to provide build-defining effects or stat boosts that shape how you play.

The old passive list had 18 items, 14 of which were evolution keys. Those 14 are now replaced by the catalysts above. The remaining 4 (Swift Boots, Magnetic Amulet, Clover, XP Tome) stay, and new passives are added.

**Generic passives have 5 levels** (unchanged from before). Players can hold **6 passives max** (unchanged), but since catalysts are consumed on evolution, late-game players typically have 4–5 passive slots available plus freed catalyst slots.

## 4. Duplicate & Slot Rules

### Weapon Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Max weapons held | 6 |
| Max weapon level | 8 |
| Duplicate base weapons | **Not possible.** Each weapon can only appear once in a run. If you hold Broad Sword, it cannot appear in level-up options again |
| Evolved weapon uniqueness | **Automatic.** Since base weapons are unique and each can only evolve once, evolved weapons are inherently unique |
| Evolution trigger | Weapon at level 8 + matching catalyst at level 3. Fires automatically and instantly |
| Catalyst consumption | Catalyst is removed from passive slots on evolution. Slot is freed |
| Multiple evolutions | A player can evolve multiple weapons in one run if they hold multiple maxed catalysts. Each evolution consumes one catalyst |

### Passive/Catalyst Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Max passives + catalysts held | 6 (shared pool) |
| Catalyst levels | 3 max |
| Generic passive levels | 5 max |
| Duplicate passives | **Not possible.** Each passive/catalyst is unique per run |
| Catalyst without matching weapon | Catalyst still provides its unique effect. It's never a dead pickup |
| Freed slot after evolution | The freed slot can be filled by any passive or catalyst from the next level-up selection |

### Level-Up Selection Pool

When the player levels up, the selection pool draws from:

1. **New weapons** (if < 6 held and unowned weapons exist)
2. **Weapon upgrades** (if any held weapon is below level 8)
3. **New catalysts** (if < 6 passives held and unowned catalysts exist)
4. **Catalyst upgrades** (if any held catalyst is below level 3)
5. **New generic passives** (if < 6 passives held and unowned passives exist)
6. **Generic passive upgrades** (if any held passive is below max level)
7. **+25 Coins** (always available)

Priority weighting:
- Catalysts matching a held weapon are weighted **2× higher** in the pool (the game subtly encourages evolution paths)
- Upgrades to held items are weighted **1.5× higher** than new pickups (finish what you started)
- +25 Coins appears as one of the 3–4 options only when the pool has more options than slots

#### 12. Revival
*Was 1 level at 1,000 coins. Now expanded.*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |

| 2 | 800 / 1,500 | +1 Revival per run. Revive at 50% HP with 2s invulnerability | 2 Revivals |

Total: 2,300 coins. Having a second revival is expensive but provides a massive safety net for hard runs.

#### 14. Piercing (NEW)
*Global starting Pierce*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 3 | 300 / 600 / 1,000 | +1 Pierce (global, applies to all projectile weapons) | +3 Pierce |

Total: 1,900 coins. This is the meta-progression path for projectile builds. A fully upgraded Ranger starts with +1 (class) +3 (meta) = 4 Pierce before any passives. Expensive but transforms how projectile weapons feel from run start.

#### 15. Haste (NEW)
*Attack Speed*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 5 | 100 / 200 / 350 / 550 / 800 | +4% Attack Speed | +20% ASPD |

Total: 2,000 coins. Attack Speed was previously unavailable in the meta store. This gives all weapon builds a slight fire rate boost.

#### 16. Tenacity (NEW)
*Crowd control resistance*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 3 | 200 / 400 / 700 | Reduce duration of slows, stuns, and debuffs on you by 15% per level | −45% CC duration |

Total: 1,300 coins. Anti-frustration upgrade. Witch curses go from 4 seconds to 2.2 seconds. Bone Golem stuns go from 0.5s to 0.275s. Doesn't prevent CC, just shortens it. Makes the late-game (where Witches and Banshees dominate) more manageable without removing the threat.

#### 17. Scavenger (NEW)
*Pickup efficiency*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 3 | 150 / 350 / 600 | Food heals +5% more Max HP per level. Coin drops are +5% more likely per level | +15% Food heal, +15% coin chance |

Total: 1,100 coins. A mid-tier upgrade that makes pickups more valuable. The food healing bonus stacks with Clover's bonus (which adds flat +3% per level). The coin chance increase is multiplicative with base drop rate.

#### 18. Catalyst Affinity (NEW)
*Evolution acceleration*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 2 | 500 / 1,200 | Catalysts appear **+15% more frequently** in level-up selections per level. At max level, catalysts also start at **level 2** when first picked up | +30% catalyst frequency, start at lv2 |

Total: 1,700 coins. Directly accelerates evolution paths. At max level, you only need one catalyst level-up (2→3) instead of two (1→3) to be evolution-ready. This is a huge time savings that makes evolutions more reliably achievable in runs where you die around minute 12–15.

#### 19. Endurance (NEW)
*Late-game survivability*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 3 | 400 / 700 / 1,100 | After minute 10, gain +3% damage reduction per level (flat, stacks with Armor). After minute 15, gain an additional +2% per level | +9% DR after 10:00, +15% DR after 15:00 |

Total: 2,200 coins. A specifically late-game focused upgrade that helps players survive the hardest part of the run without making the early game easier. At max level: +9% DR from 10:00–14:59, +15% DR from 15:00 onward. This is the "I want to see endgame content" upgrade path.

#### 20. Arsenal (NEW)
*Starting weapon boost*

| Lvls | Cost | Effect | Max |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 3 | 250 / 500 / 900 | Your starting weapon begins each run at **+1 level** per Arsenal level | Starting weapon at Lv 4 |

Total: 1,650 coins. Start the game with your class weapon at level 2/3/4 instead of 1. At max Arsenal, your starting weapon does `base × 1.10^3 = 1.33×` damage from the first second. Doesn't affect any other weapons you pick up — only the starting weapon. This makes early game smoother without affecting the late-game power ceiling.

**Grand Total to Fully Upgrade: ~34,440 coins**

This is roughly **2× the old total** (was ~16,700). With the improved coin payouts from v2.0 (500–750 per clear, 120–250 average), full completion takes approximately:
- **50–70 full clears** (experienced player)
- **120–180 average runs** (mixed outcomes)

This provides ~40–60 hours of meaningful meta-progression, which is appropriate for the genre. Players should unlock the key upgrades they care about within 10–15 hours, with full completion being a long-term goal.
