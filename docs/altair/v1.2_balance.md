# Altair — Balance Patch v1.2: Weapon Meta & Class Parity

> **Scope:** Targeted nerfs to War Axe and Garlic; buffs to underperforming starting weapons; class tuning to close the gap between Berserker and everyone else.
> **Context:** Post-v2.0 overhaul. The game is harder and more fun, but the melee/aura meta has emerged as dominant. War Axe's 360° cleave + Garlic's passive aura lets players (especially Berserker) stand in place and clear everything with zero aiming. Projectile-based classes — particularly Ranger — feel underpowered by comparison because they demand directional play for less reward.

## 1. Problem Breakdown

### Why War Axe Is Broken

The War Axe has three properties that compound into dominance:

1. **360° coverage.** Every other melee or projectile weapon requires facing or aiming. The Axe hits everything around the player unconditionally. In a game where enemies come from all directions, this is an enormous advantage.
2. **High base damage (24).** Even after the v2.0 nerf from 28, it's still the second-highest base damage weapon in the game. Combined with the 360° arc, its effective DPS-per-threat is unmatched.
3. **8-target cap is too generous.** In practice, most swings hit 6–8 enemies because the 80px range means only nearby enemies are hit — and nearby enemies are the ones that matter. The cap rarely activates as a real constraint.

The result: War Axe alone handles Tier 1–3 enemies for the first 8+ minutes. Pair it with Garlic and the player has full 360° coverage at two different ranges with zero input required.

### Why Garlic Is Broken

Even after the v2.0 nerf (3 dmg/tick, 60px radius, 0.75s ticks), Garlic's fundamental problem remains: **it requires zero player input and never misses.** It's a passive damage aura that hits everything in range, every tick, forever. For any enemy that gets close, Garlic is guaranteed chip damage + knockback. It's never a bad pickup regardless of build, and at higher levels it becomes a permanent safety net.

The 15px knockback per tick is the hidden offender. At 0.75s tick rate, enemies within 60px are pushed 20 px/s outward. Slow enemies (Shamblers at 85 px/s) have their effective approach speed reduced to 65 px/s — a 24% slow equivalent, for free, with no cooldown, on every enemy simultaneously.

### Why Berserker Benefits Most

Berserker starts with War Axe and has Blood Rage (bonus Might from missing HP). Since both War Axe and Garlic are melee-range tools, the Berserker's playstyle of standing in danger and tanking hits is perfectly served. Blood Rage's damage bonus directly amplifies the already-high War Axe base damage, and the Berserker's 140 HP pool gives room to take hits while the aura + cleave delete everything nearby.

No other class has this synergy. Every other starting weapon requires aiming or positioning, meaning their class abilities are partially wasted on movement instead of combat.

### Why Ranger Feels Worst

The Iron Shortbow fires in the player's **facing direction**. In a game where enemies surround you, a directional weapon means you're only ever hitting threats from one direction. The v2.0 nerf to Pierce 1 made this worse — arrows hit 2 enemies max and disappear, so even the enemies you're aiming at take limited damage. Meanwhile the Ranger's entire stat line (Move Speed, Proj Speed, Proj Count) is built around projectile play that just doesn't pay off compared to standing still with an Axe.

## 2. War Axe Nerfs

The goal is to keep the War Axe's 360° identity but make it clearly a slow, committal weapon with windows of vulnerability — not a passive damage field.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Base Damage | 24 | **20** | Meaningful reduction. Still hits hard per swing but DPS drops significantly |
| Cooldown | 2.0s | **2.3s** | Slower. Creates a gap between swings where enemies can close in |
| Range | 80px | **70px** | Tighter radius. Player must be right in the thick of it |
| Max Targets | 8 | **6** | Stricter cap. Dense swarms partially survive |
| **Windup (NEW)** | None | **0.25s** | The Axe now has a brief windup before the swing connects. Player is animation-locked during windup — they can still move but at 60% speed. This is the key change: the Axe is no longer an instant 360° clear. There's a commitment window |

### Windup Details

The 0.25s windup means:
- At 2.3s cooldown, the effective attack cycle is: 2.05s idle → 0.25s windup (slow) → hit → repeat.
- During windup, the player's move speed is reduced to **60% of current speed**. This means the Berserker (205 base) drops to 123 px/s during windup — slower than Skeleton Warriors (100 px/s base + scaling).
- Visual telegraph: the character raises the axe with a brief flash before the swing, giving the player clear feedback on timing.
- This doesn't feel clunky for the Berserker player — it feels *heavy* and *committal*, which matches the class fantasy.

### War Axe Level Scaling Adjustment

Levels 2 and 5 no longer grant +1 max target. Instead:
- **Level 2:** +10px range (70→80px)
- **Level 5:** +10px range (80→90px) and windup reduced to **0.2s**

This means the Axe gradually improves its range and responsiveness, but never returns to the old "instant 360° kill zone" feel.

### DPS Comparison (Before and After)

Assuming Might 1.0, level 1:

| | v2.0 | v2.1 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Damage per swing | 24 | 20 | −17% |
| Swings per second | 0.50 | 0.43 | −13% |
| DPS (single target) | 12.0 | 8.7 | **−28%** |
| DPS (6 targets) | 72.0 | 52.2 | −28% |
| DPS (8 targets) | 96.0 | 52.2 | **−46%** (cap reduced) |

At level 8 with 1.3× Might:

| | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|---|---|---|
| Damage per swing | 24 × 1.95 × 1.3 = 60.8 | 20 × 1.95 × 1.3 = 50.7 |
| Max target DPS | 60.8 × 8 / 2.0 = 243.2 | 50.7 × 6 / 2.3 = 132.3 |

**Effective AoE DPS nearly halved** at max level. This is the intended magnitude of the nerf.

## 3. Garlic Nerfs

Garlic needs to be repositioned from "passive damage that kills things" to "a defensive breathing tool that softens enemies for real weapons to finish."

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Base Damage | 3/tick | **2/tick** | Nearly halved from v1. Garlic is not a kill weapon |
| Tick Rate | 0.75s | **1.0s** | Slower ticks. Base DPS: 2.0 (was 4.0 in v2.0, was 10.0 in v1) |
| Radius | 60px | 60px | Unchanged |
| Knockback | 15px/tick | **8px/tick** | Drastically reduced. Enemies actually reach you now |
| **Max Targets (NEW)** | Unlimited | **10** | Prevents clearing entire swarms passively |
| **Damage Falloff (NEW)** | None | Enemies within inner 30px take full damage. Enemies in outer 30–60px take **50% damage** (1/tick) | Rewards being surrounded (dangerous) over keeping enemies at arm's length |

### Knockback Math (v2.1)

At 8px per tick, 1.0s tick rate = 8 px/s pushback.

| Enemy | Base Speed | Net Approach Speed (v2.0) | Net Approach Speed (v2.1) |
|-------|-----------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Shambler (85) | 85 px/s | 65 px/s (−24%) | 77 px/s (−9%) |
| Bat (170) | 170 px/s | 150 px/s (−12%) | 162 px/s (−5%) |
| Werewolf (90) | 90 px/s | 70 px/s (−22%) | 82 px/s (−9%) |
| Death Knight (120) | 120 px/s | 100 px/s (−17%) | 112 px/s (−7%) |

Garlic's knockback is now negligible against anything that isn't already slow. It's no longer a soft crowd-control tool — it's a mild annoyance to enemies, which is correct for a 0-input passive.

### Garlic Level Scaling Adjustment

- **Level 2:** +1 damage/tick (2→3)
- **Level 5:** +15px radius (60→75px)
- Other levels: +1 damage/tick as normal

At max level (5 upgrades from level-ups, ~+3 damage): 5 damage/tick, 75px radius, 1.0s ticks = 5 DPS inner, 2.5 DPS outer. This is **supplemental** damage, not a primary weapon.

### Garlic's New Role

Garlic is now a **comfort passive** that:
- Provides small chip damage to soften enemies for real weapons
- Gives minor knockback to create micro-spacing
- Has zero aim requirement (its only remaining strength)
- Is never worth building around as a primary damage source

Players who want garlic's safety should know they're trading a weapon slot for comfort, not DPS.

## 5. Berserker Class Nerfs

With the War Axe nerfed, Berserker also needs targeted stat adjustments so it doesn't just pick up other weapons and still dominate through raw stats.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Max HP | 140 | **130** | Berserker had the most HP in the game. 130 is still highest but the margin over Knight (120) is smaller |
| HP Regen | 0.3 | **0.2** | Less free sustain. Blood Rage's risk should feel risky |
| Move Speed | 205 | 200 | Brought to average. Berserker shouldn't also be fast |

### Blood Rage Adjustment

The formula stays `bonus = 0.6 × (1 - currentHp/maxHp)` but the Attack Speed bonus below 25% is removed entirely:

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|------|------|------|
| Bonus Might formula | 0.6 × (1 − hp/maxHp) | 0.6 × (1 − hp/maxHp) (unchanged) |
| ASPD below 25% HP | +15% | **Removed** |

The free Attack Speed at low HP was a hidden multiplier that made War Axe's slow cooldown less relevant. Removing it means the Berserker gets raw Might from Blood Rage (which is the class identity) but doesn't also get to swing faster.

### Savage Slam Adjustment

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Damage | 90 | 90 | Unchanged |
| Radius | 150px | 150px | Unchanged |
| Cooldown | 30s | **35s** | Slightly longer between slams |
| Invulnerability | 0.6s | **0.4s** | Less free safety during the leap |

## 6. Iron Shortbow Buffs (Ranger)

The Shortbow needs to reward its aiming requirement with meaningfully higher effective DPS than passive weapons. The core issue is that directional weapons must outperform omnidirectional ones *when aimed correctly* to justify the skill demand.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Base Damage | 10 | **12** | Meaningful bump. Each arrow hits harder |
| Cooldown | 0.9s | **0.75s** | Faster fire rate. The bow should feel snappy |
| Pierce | 1 (2 enemies) | **2 (3 enemies)** | Arrows pass through 2 enemies and hit a 3rd. This is the key buff — directional pierce should be more generous than omnidirectional AoE because you're only hitting one line of enemies |
| Base Projectile Count | 1 | **1** | Unchanged (Ranger no longer starts with +1 from class stats; that was removed in v2.0) |
| Projectile Speed | 350 px/s | **400 px/s** | Faster arrows = less leading required, feels more responsive |
| Range | 400px | **450px** | Slightly longer range. Reward for being a ranged weapon |
| **Crit Chance (NEW)** | None | **8% base** (affected by Luck) | Crits deal **1.5× damage**. This is unique to the Shortbow — it's the only base weapon with innate crit. The Ranger's higher Luck investment pays off through this weapon |

### Crit System (Shortbow-Specific)

Crits only apply to the Iron Shortbow and its evolution (Storm Bow). This is not a global system — it's a weapon trait.

```
crit_chance = 0.08 × luck
crit_damage = base_hit × 1.5
```

At 1.0× Luck: 8% crit chance. At 1.3× Luck (Clover lv5): 10.4%. At max meta Luck (1.5×) + Clover: `1.5 × 1.3 = 1.95×` Luck → 15.6% crit chance.

This gives the Ranger a unique scaling path that rewards investment in Luck — a stat that no other class particularly wants for DPS.

### DPS Comparison: Shortbow vs War Axe (v2.1)

Single target, level 1, Might 1.0:

| Weapon | Damage/hit | Hits/sec | Single Target DPS | Notes |
|--------|-----------|---------|------------------|-------|
| War Axe (v2.1) | 20 | 0.43 | 8.7 | Always hits (360°) |
| Shortbow (v2.1) | 12 | 1.33 | 16.0 | Must aim. +8% crit raises effective to ~16.96 |

**The Shortbow now deals nearly 2× the single-target DPS of the War Axe when aimed.** This is the correct ratio. The War Axe's advantage is hitting 6 enemies at once without aiming. The Shortbow's advantage is focused damage output and reach. Both are valid, neither dominates.

With Pierce 2 and max targets in a line:

| Weapon | Max targets | Total DPS across targets |
|--------|------------|------------------------|
| War Axe | 6 (melee) | 52.2 |
| Shortbow | 3 (line) | 48.0 |

Comparable total DPS but the Shortbow requires aiming and enemies must be lined up. Fair tradeoff.

### Iron Shortbow Level Scaling

- **Level 2:** +1 Pierce (2→3, hits 4 enemies)
- **Level 5:** +2% crit chance (8%→10% base), +1 projectile

These breakpoints make the Shortbow feel meaningfully stronger at mid-game without becoming overpowered.

### Storm Bow (Evolution) Buff

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Arrow count bonus | +50% | +50% | Unchanged |
| Pierce | 4 | **5** | Slightly more generous for the evolved form |
| Lightning trails | 3 dmg/tick, 1s, 20px | 4 dmg/tick, 1.2s, 25px | Slightly stronger trails to reward the Ranger's investment |
| **Crit chance bonus** | None | **+5%** (additive to base) | Evolved Shortbow has 13% base crit (before Luck). Crits now deal **1.75×** (up from 1.5×) |

## 8. Arcane Bolt Buffs (Arcanist)

Arcane Bolt's single-target homing is correct design (v2.0 was right to remove pierce), but the damage and fire rate need to better compensate for being single-target.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Base Damage | 15 | **17** | Bump. Single-target homing should hit harder per bolt |
| Cooldown | 1.2s | **1.05s** | Faster fire. Homing bolts should feel like a steady stream |
| Tracking radius | 300px | **350px** | Slightly longer range |
| Turn rate | 180°/s | **220°/s** | Better tracking. Misses fast targets less often |
| Pierce | 0 | 0 | Stays single-target |
| **Splash (NEW)** | None | On kill, the bolt **splashes** for **40% of its damage** in a **40px radius** around the target | This is the key addition. Arcane Bolt is still single-target in terms of pierce, but killing an enemy rewards you with a small AoE pop. Encourages targeting weak enemies in clusters — a different skill expression than aiming |

### Arcane Bolt Level Scaling

- **Level 2:** Splash radius +10px (40→50px)
- **Level 5:** Splash damage 40%→55%, +1 additional bolt per cast (fires 2 bolts at 2 nearest enemies)

The level 5 breakpoint is significant — the Arcanist goes from one bolt to two, effectively doubling their target coverage. But each bolt is still single-target with conditional splash.

### Arcane Barrage (Evolution) Adjustment

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|------|------|------|
| Bolts per cast | 2 | **3** (restoring the original, now that base bolt is properly nerfed) |
| Explosion radius | 60px, 5 enemy cap | 55px, **4 enemy cap** |
| Splash on kill | N/A | Inherited from base (40% splash on kill, stacks with explosion) |

## 13. Broad Sword Buffs (Knight)

The Broad Sword was caught in the crossfire of melee nerfs aimed at the War Axe. It's a directional melee weapon (100° arc) which means it requires facing — it should be rewarded for that, not treated the same as the 360° Axe.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Base Damage | 18 | **19** | Slight bump |
| Cooldown | 1.3s | **1.2s** | Restoring v1.0 speed. The Sword is faster than the Axe as its tradeoff for narrower arc |
| Arc | 100° | **110°** | Slightly wider. More forgiving without approaching 360° territory |
| Range | 90px | **95px** | Marginal reach increase |
| **Block (NEW)** | None | During the 0.1s after a swing connects with an enemy, the Knight takes **25% reduced damage** from all sources. Visual: brief shield flash on hit | This is a small defensive bonus unique to the Broad Sword. It rewards aggressive melee play — you swing into enemies and get a tiny damage reduction window. Over a fight, this adds up to significant effective HP. Thematic for the Knight's tanky identity |

### Broad Sword Level Scaling

- **Level 2:** Block damage reduction 25%→30%
- **Level 5:** Arc widened to 120°, +5px range (95→100px)

### Radiant Claymore (Evolution) Adjustment

## 14. Non-Starting Weapon Adjustments

A few pickable weapons need minor tuning to maintain parity after the starting weapon changes.

### Throwing Daggers — Small Buff

Daggers were overnerfed in v2.0. With 0 pierce and 6 base damage, they couldn't compete with any starting weapon.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|------|------|------|
| Base Damage | 6 | **7** |
| Cooldown | 0.7s | **0.65s** |
| Pierce | 0 | **1** (hits 2 enemies) |
| Proj Count | 2 base | **3 base** |

Daggers are now a "spray and pray" weapon with decent trash-clear. 3 daggers × 1.33/s × 7 damage × 2 targets each = 56 total DPS across targets when enemies are clustered. Competitive but requires facing.

### Lightning Ring — Small Buff

Lightning felt too slow at 3.0s cooldown for a single-target auto-strike.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |

DPS: 8.8 (was 6.7). Still single-target, but actually meaningful as a secondary weapon.

### Holy Water — No Changes

In a good spot post v2.0. Area denial that doesn't outperform Toxic Flask.

### Fire Wand — No Changes

Single-target sniper role is well-defined and balanced.

### Runic Orbs — Small Buff

Orbitals felt underwhelming with the 0.5s per-enemy hit cooldown.

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |

## 17. Design Intent Summary

### The New Weapon Hierarchy

After v2.1, every starting weapon should have a clear identity and competitive DPS when played to its strengths:

| Weapon | Identity | Skill Demand | Best At | Weakness |
|--------|----------|-------------|---------|----------|
| Broad Sword | Fast directional melee | Low-Medium | Reliable DPS, defensive block | Narrow arc, short range |
| Arcane Bolt | Homing single-target | Low | Consistent damage, kill-splash | Low AoE, slow against hordes |
| Iron Shortbow | Aimed ranged pierce | **High** | Single-target/line DPS, crits | Directional only, requires aiming |
| Toxic Flask | Smart-lobbed area denial | Low-Medium | Zone control, slowing clusters | Low burst, DoT-reliant |
| War Axe | Slow 360° melee | Low | Surrounded melee, crowd thin | Slow, windup commitment, low single-target |
| Soul Siphon | Sustained single-target beam | Low | Drain+slow 1 target, skeleton synergy | Tiny range, no AoE |
| Temporal Shard | Skill-shot boomerang | Medium-High | Line clear, return damage bonus | Must position for double-hit |
| Crimson Whip | Wide medium-range lash | Medium | Multi-target in front, lifesteal | Directional, medium range |

### Class Power Budget

Every class should be roughly equal in total power but distribute it differently:

| Class | Survivability | DPS Potential | Ease of Play | Unique Niche |
|-------|--------------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Knight | ★★★★ | ★★★ | ★★★★★ | Tanky baseline, block mechanic |
| Arcanist | ★★ | ★★★★ | ★★★★ | Burst windows, splash kills |
| Ranger | ★★★ | ★★★★★ | ★★ | Highest DPS ceiling, crit scaling |
| Plague Doctor | ★★★ | ★★★ | ★★★ | Area denial, crowd control |
| Berserker | ★★★ | ★★★★ | ★★★ | Risk/reward melee |
| Necromancer | ★★ | ★★★ (★★★★ w/ army) | ★★★ | Army management, skeletal swarm |
| Chronomancer | ★★★★ | ★★★ | ★★★ | Time control, safety |
| Hemomancer | ★★ | ★★★★ | ★★ | Lifesteal sustain, risk/reward |

The Ranger now has the **highest DPS ceiling** in the game but also one of the lowest ease-of-play ratings. This is the correct tradeoff: aim-heavy weapons should be the strongest when mastered, weakest when played poorly. The Berserker drops from dominant to competitive — still strong in melee but no longer the clear best pick.
