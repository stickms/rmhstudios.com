The fourteen maps gathered here offer a geographic tour of Indonesian history from deep geological time to the twenty-first century. Arranged in four thematic groups, they move from the physical shape of the archipelago and its biogeographic fault-lines, through the prehistoric migrations and trade networks that first made the islands a connected world, to the competing polities of the classical and early-modern periods, and finally to the colonial transformations and contemporary borders that define Indonesia today. Taken together they are intended as a visual companion to the main text, allowing readers to situate events in space and to trace the recurring role that geography has played as both constraint and enabler of Indonesian history.
