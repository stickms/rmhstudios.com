## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Demokrasi Terpimpin | "Guided Democracy" — the political system proclaimed by Sukarno after the decree of 5 July 1959, concentrating executive power in the president and governing alongside the army and PKI as the major extraparliamentary forces. Lasted until 1966–67. |
| Dwikora | "People's Dual Command" — Sukarno's May 1964 mobilization proclamation escalating the Konfrontasi against Malaysia, calling for Indonesian volunteers to "crush Malaysia." |
| MANIPOL-USDEK | The official state ideology of Guided Democracy: *MANIPOL* = *Manifesto Politik* (Political Manifesto, 1959); *USDEK* = UUD 1945, Sosialisme Indonesia, Demokrasi Terpimpin, Ekonomi Terpimpin, Kepribadian Indonesia. Mandatory study in all schools and for civil servants. |
| NASAKOM | The Sukarno-era ideological formula combining *Nasionalisme* (nationalism), *Agama* (religion), and *Komunisme* (communism) — intended to unite secular nationalists, Islamic organizations, and the PKI within Guided Democracy's coalition. |
| Pepera | *Penentuan Pendapat Rakyat* — the Act of Free Choice conducted in 1969 in West Irian, in which 1,025 hand-picked representatives voted unanimously to remain with Indonesia; widely condemned by international scholars as a sham plebiscite. |
| PKI | *Partai Komunis Indonesia* — the Indonesian Communist Party, founded 1920, revived under D.N. Aidit from 1951, and by 1965 the world's largest non-ruling communist party with approximately three million members. Destroyed in the 1965–66 mass killings. |
| PRRI | *Pemerintah Revolusioner Republik Indonesia* ("Revolutionary Government of the Republic of Indonesia") — the rebel government proclaimed in West Sumatra on 15 February 1958 by dissident military officers and politicians; allied with the Permesta movement in Sulawesi; suppressed by mid-1961. |
| Trikora | *Tri Komando Rakyat* ("People's Triple Command") — Sukarno's December 1961 proclamation launching the military campaign to recover West Irian from the Netherlands, comprising three demands: prevent a Dutch puppet state, raise the Indonesian flag, and mobilize the people for liberation. |
