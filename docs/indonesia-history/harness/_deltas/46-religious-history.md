## GLOSSARY
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Siwa-Buddha | The court theology of late Hindu-Buddhist Java (especially Majapahit), in which Shiva and the Buddha were understood as complementary aspects of the supreme divine reality. Detailed in the Nagarakretagama (1365). |
| wali songo | "Nine Saints" — revered Sufi teachers traditionally credited with the Islamization of Java in the 15th–16th centuries. Each has a recognized grave site serving as a major pilgrimage destination. |
| santri | In Geertz's typology, Javanese Muslims characterized by strict Islamic observance and mosque-centered life; also refers non-typologically to pesantren students. Critiqued by Woodward and Ricklefs for reifying a political distinction. |
| kebatinan | Family of Javanese spiritual practices centered on cultivating inner refinement (rasa) and alignment with the cosmic order, drawing on animist, Hindu-Buddhist, and Sufi elements. Categorized by the Indonesian state as kepercayaan rather than agama until 2017. |
| Ketuhanan Yang Maha Esa | "Belief in the One Almighty God" — the first principle of Pancasila, adopted August 18, 1945, after the removal of the seven-words clause obligating Muslims to sharia. Designed to accommodate all Indonesian religious traditions simultaneously. |
| penghayat kepercayaan | "Indigenous belief practitioners" — adherents of non-recognized spiritual traditions, estimated at 12 million people. Denied full civil recognition until the 2017 Constitutional Court ruling (Decision No. 97/PUU-XIV/2016). |
| Islam Nusantara | "Archipelago Islam" — NU-promoted concept presenting Indonesia's historically developed synthesis of Islamic teaching with local cultural traditions as a positive theological achievement, emphasizing pluralism, tolerance, and contextual legal reasoning. Formally endorsed at the 33rd NU Congress (2015). |
| Piagam Jakarta | Jakarta Charter — document drawn up June 22, 1945 containing the Pancasila principles plus seven words (dengan kewajiban menjalankan syariat Islam bagi pemeluk-pemeluknya) imposing Islamic law on Muslims; the seven words were removed on August 18, 1945. |
| abangan | In Geertz's typology, Javanese Muslims characterized by syncretic blending of animist, Hindu-Buddhist, and Islamic elements centered on the communal feast (slametan). Critiqued as a normative external label rather than a self-ascription. |
| pesantren | Islamic boarding school, the primary educational institution of the NU traditionalist network; serves as the organizational heartbeat of Indonesian Sunni Islam. |
| HKBP | Huria Kristen Batak Protestan — Batak Christian Protestant Church, founded from Rhenish Mission work of Ludwig Ingwer Nommensen; one of the world's largest Lutheran churches, with approximately 4 million members. |
