## GLOSSARY

New terms to append to glossary-ledger.md (keep alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| cartaz | A sea-pass issued by Portuguese port authorities to Asian merchant vessels, authorizing transit without interference. Ships without a valid cartaz faced seizure. The primary enforcement mechanism of the Estado da India's Indian Ocean toll system. |
| casados | "Married men" — the class of Portuguese settlers in Asia who had taken local wives with official encouragement. Their Eurasian households conducted inter-Asian trade networks that extended Portuguese commercial reach far beyond what Crown ships could achieve. |
| Estado da India | "State of India" — the administrative apparatus governing all Portuguese territories, posts, and naval forces east of the Cape of Good Hope, formally established in 1505 and governed from Goa. |
| feitoria | A Portuguese fortified trading post or factory in an Asian port, staffed by a Crown-appointed feitor (factor) who collected customs duties and issued trade licences. The commercial node of the Estado da India network. |
| Padroado | The system of royal patronage by which the Portuguese Crown held the right to nominate Catholic clergy and establish churches in its overseas territories, granted by papal bulls from the 1450s. Made the Portuguese state the official instrument of Catholic mission across the Indian Ocean. |
| Papia Kristang | A Portuguese-based creole language ("speak Christian") developed by the Eurasian casado communities of Malacca. The only extant Portuguese-derived creole in Southeast Asia, still spoken in Melaka's "Portuguese Settlement." |
