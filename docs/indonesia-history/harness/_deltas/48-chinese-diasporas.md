## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Baperki | Badan Permusyawaratan Kewarganegaraan Indonesia ("Consultative Body on Indonesian Citizenship"), founded 1954; the main Chinese-Indonesian political organisation under Sukarno, advocating integration as full citizens; banned after October 1965. |
| cukong | Hokkien term for a Chinese business patron; used in the New Order context for Chinese-Indonesian entrepreneurs who provided capital and commercial expertise to Suharto's circle in exchange for political protection and monopoly access. |
| kapitan (Kapitan Cina / Kapitein der Chinezen) | Colonial-era Chinese community headman appointed by the VOC and later Dutch authorities to mediate between the colonial government and the Chinese community; the first was Souw Beng Kong (Batavia, 1619). |
| Nanyang | "Southern Seas" in Chinese; the term used by Chinese emigrants to refer to maritime Southeast Asia, their zone of migration and commerce. |
| passenstelsel | "Pass system" in Dutch; colonial regulation requiring Chinese (and other Vreemde Oosterlingen) to carry travel passes when moving outside their designated residential quarters. |
| pecinan | Indonesian term for a Chinese residential quarter (Chinatown) in a colonial or contemporary Indonesian city; the Glodok district of Jakarta is the most famous. |
| peranakan | "Locally born"; ethnic Chinese who had settled for several generations in the Indonesian archipelago and adopted substantial aspects of local (Javanese or Malay) language, dress, and culture, while retaining Chinese surnames and some ceremonial practices. |
| SBKRI | Surat Bukti Kewarganegaraan Republik Indonesia ("Certificate of Proof of Indonesian Citizenship"); a document required specifically of Chinese Indonesians under the New Order, functioning as a discriminatory bureaucratic barrier to schooling, banking, and employment; abolished by the 2006 Citizenship Law. |
| totok | Ethnic Chinese Indonesians of more recent migration or who maintained Chinese-language use, cultural orientation toward China, and homeland-facing identity; contrasted with peranakan. |
| Vreemde Oosterlingen | Dutch: "Foreign Orientals"; the colonial legal category grouping Chinese, Arabs, and Indians in the Netherlands East Indies into an intermediate legal tier between Europeans (Europeanen) and indigenous Indonesians (Inlanders), codified from 1818. |
| wijkenstelsel | Dutch residential-segregation system requiring Chinese and other Vreemde Oosterlingen to live within designated quarters (wijken) in colonial cities. |
