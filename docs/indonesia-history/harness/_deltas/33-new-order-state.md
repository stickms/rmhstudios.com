## GLOSSARY

New terms for `glossary-ledger.md` (keep alphabetical; terms already in ledger — *Dwifungsi*, *Pancasila*, *Reformasi* — are not repeated):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Asas tunggal | "Sole foundation" — the New Order requirement, enacted in Law No. 8 of 1985, that all Indonesian social and political organisations adopt Pancasila as their only ideological basis, displacing Islamic or other foundations. |
| Bapak Pembangunan | "Father of Development" — honorary title conferred on Suharto by the MPR in 1982; the focal point of a personality cult centred on developmental achievement. |
| Berkeley Mafia | Informal designation for the cohort of US-trained Indonesian economists — led by Widjojo Nitisastro — who managed New Order economic policy through Bappenas and the finance ministry. |
| BIMAS | *Bimbingan Massal* (Mass Guidance) — the New Order agricultural extension programme that distributed high-yielding rice varieties and inputs to village farmers, enabling self-sufficiency by 1984. |
| Cukong | An ethnic-Chinese business partner of a politically connected indigenous official or military officer in the New Order's patronage economy; the term denotes a structural role rather than mere pejorative. |
| Golkar | *Golongan Karya* ("Functional Groups") — the New Order's electoral vehicle, which won between 62 and 75 percent of the vote in all six general elections from 1971 to 1997. |
| KKN | *Korupsi, Kolusi, Nepotisme* — the triad of corruption, collusion, and nepotism that characterised the Suharto regime's political economy; the central slogan of the 1998 Reformasi movement. |
| Kopkamtib | *Komando Operasi Pemulihan Keamanan dan Ketertiban* — Operational Command for the Restoration of Security and Order; the New Order's primary extra-legal security instrument (1965–1988), replaced by Bakorstanas. |
| Massa mengambang | "Floating mass" — the New Order policy that treated rural Indonesians as requiring insulation from partisan politics, used to restrict opposition parties to subdistrict level while Golkar retained village-level access. |
| Monoloyalitas | New Order requirement (Government Regulation No. 6/1970) that civil servants channel all political activity exclusively through Golkar, enforced through mandatory KORPRI membership. |
| Orde Baru | "New Order" — Suharto's self-designation for his regime (1966–1998), contrasted explicitly with Sukarno's "Old Order." |
| P4 | *Pedoman Penghayatan dan Pengamalan Pancasila* — Guide to Experiencing and Practising Pancasila; a mandatory state indoctrination programme (MPR Resolution No. 2/1978) requiring rote memorisation of 36 formulaic precepts by civil servants and university students. |
| Petrus | *Penembakan Misterius* ("Mysterious Shootings") — a campaign of extrajudicial killings by Indonesian Army death squads targeting suspected criminals, 1983–85; Suharto acknowledged authorising the killings in his 1988 autobiography. |
| REPELITA | *Rencana Pembangunan Lima Tahun* — Indonesia's five-year development plans (I–V, 1969–1993), designed by the Berkeley Mafia technocrats through Bappenas. |
| Yayasan | Charitable foundation; under the New Order, dozens of yayasan ostensibly devoted to welfare functioned as compulsory collection vehicles for the Suharto family's personal enrichment. |
