## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Act of Free Choice (*Pepera*) | The July–August 1969 process in which 1,025 hand-picked Papuan representatives voted unanimously for integration with Indonesia, replacing a proposed universal plebiscite; widely criticized as fraudulent. |
| ITE Law (*UU ITE*) | *Undang-Undang Informasi dan Transaksi Elektronik* — Indonesia's Electronic Information and Transactions Law (2008, amended 2016, 2023), whose broadly drawn criminal provisions against "defamatory" or "offensive" online content have been used extensively against journalists, activists, and critics. |
| KKR (*Komisi Kebenaran dan Rekonsiliasi*) | Indonesia's Truth and Reconciliation Commission, authorized by Law No. 27 of 2004 but struck down by the Constitutional Court in December 2006 because its reparations provisions required victims to consent to perpetrators' amnesties. |
| KPK (*Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi*) | The Corruption Eradication Commission, established 2002; significantly weakened by Law No. 19/2019, which placed it under executive oversight and created a presidentially-appointed supervisory board. |
| KUHP (*Kitab Undang-Undang Hukum Pidana*) | Indonesia's Criminal Code; the revised KUHP passed December 2022 replaces colonial-era Dutch penal law and enters force January 2026. Contested provisions include presidential-insult articles, expanded blasphemy, and criminalisation of consensual extramarital sex. |
| OPM (*Organisasi Papua Merdeka*) | Free Papua Movement, founded 1965; armed wing now operating as TPNPB (West Papua National Liberation Army). |
| Petrus (*penembakan misterius*) | "Mysterious shootings" — the Suharto government's extrajudicial killing campaign against alleged criminals, 1982–85; acknowledged by Suharto himself as deliberate policy ("shock therapy"); classified as gross human rights violations by Komnas HAM in 2012. |
| PPHAM Team | *Tim Penyelesaian Non-Yudisial Pelanggaran Hak Asasi Manusia yang Berat Masa Lalu* — the non-judicial team established by Presidential Regulation 17/2022, whose report led to Jokowi's January 2023 acknowledgment of 12 past gross violations. |
| Tim Mawar (Rose Team) | A covert unit of Kopassus Group IV that abducted 23 political activists in 1997–98; 13 remain missing. |
| TPNPB | *Tentara Pembebasan Nasional Papua Barat* — West Papua National Liberation Army, the armed wing of the OPM/Free Papua Movement; designated a terrorist organization by Indonesia in 2021. |
| ULMWP | United Liberation Movement for West Papua, founded December 7, 2014 in Vanuatu; an unarmed political umbrella body pursuing independence through diplomacy, led by Benny Wenda in exile. |
