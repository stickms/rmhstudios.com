## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| babad | A Javanese court chronicle, typically in verse (tembang), recounting dynastic history as a blend of genealogy, mythology, cosmological legitimation, and occasional historical detail. The most influential is the Babad Tanah Jawi ("Chronicle of the Land of Java"). |
| hikayat | A Malay prose narrative or chronicle, typically recording the history and genealogy of a royal court. The Hikayat Raja-Raja Pasai is the oldest surviving Malay chronicle. |
| kakawin | A genre of Old Javanese court poetry composed in Sanskrit quantitative metres. Includes the Nagarakretagama and the Kakawin Sutasoma, source of the motto Bhinneka Tunggal Ika. |
| KITLV | Koninklijk Instituut voor Taal-, Land- en Volkenkunde (Royal Netherlands Institute for Southeast Asian and Caribbean Studies), founded in Leiden in 1851; the principal institutional home for Dutch scholarship on Indonesia. |
| lontar | A palm-leaf manuscript, made from dried strips of the Borassus palm. The principal vehicle for Old Javanese and Balinese literary and religious texts. |
| prasasti | A Sanskrit-derived term for an inscription on stone, copper plate, or bronze, recording royal edicts, land grants, genealogies, or religious dedications; the primary source base for the Hindu-Buddhist period. |
| yupa | A Vedic ritual post or pillar; specifically the seven Sanskrit-inscribed pillars of Kutai (c. 400 CE), the earliest dated writing from the Indonesian archipelago. |
| Indonesianisasi | The "Indonesianization" of historical scholarship — the political and intellectual project of transferring control of Indonesian history-writing from Dutch and Western institutions to Indonesian scholars and institutions. |
| ANRI | Arsip Nasional Republik Indonesia (National Archives of the Republic of Indonesia, Jakarta) — the principal repository for Indonesian state records, including colonial-era documents transferred from the Netherlands. |
| LIPI | Lembaga Ilmu Pengetahuan Indonesia (Indonesian Institute of Sciences), founded 1956; the state body overseeing scientific and historical research, whose independence was constrained during the New Order. |
