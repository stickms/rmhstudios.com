## GLOSSARY

New terms for glossary-ledger.md (alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| entrepôt | A port that serves primarily as a redistribution centre rather than a production site; goods from multiple origins are assembled, sorted, and resold. Malacca was the archetypal pre-modern entrepôt of the spice trade. |
| *Estado da India* | "State of India" — the Portuguese maritime empire in Asia (1505–1961), centred on fortified trading posts (*feitorias*) from East Africa to Japan, combining licensed trade with naval coercion. |
| *feitoria* | A Portuguese fortified trading post (*factory*) established at key maritime nodes from East Africa to Japan; the basic unit of the Estado da India's commercial-military system. |
| *jong* | A large Javanese or Malay sailing vessel used in the inter-island spice trade before the seventeenth century; could carry several hundred tonnes of cargo. |
| *mudaraba* | Islamic commercial partnership contract in which one party supplies capital and another supplies labour or expertise; profits are shared by agreement, losses borne by the capital provider. The *mudaraba* enabled long-distance Indian Ocean trade across cultural and linguistic barriers. |
| *shahbandar* | Literally "harbour-master" (Persian). In Malacca and other Southeast Asian port polities, the official responsible for supervising a specific community of foreign merchants, collecting duties, and mediating disputes. Malacca operated four *shahbandar* posts, one per major trader community. |
