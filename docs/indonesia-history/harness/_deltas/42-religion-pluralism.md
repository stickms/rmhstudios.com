## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Islam Nusantara | "Archipelago Islam"; a theological and civilizational concept articulated by Nahdlatul Ulama describing the historically developed synthesis of Islamic teaching with Indonesian local cultural traditions, emphasizing tolerance, pluralism, and contextual legal reasoning. Not a new sect or school of jurisprudence but a methodology of cultural integration. |
| Nahdlatul Ulama | "Revival of Religious Scholars" (Arabic/Indonesian); Indonesia's largest Islamic organization, founded January 31, 1926 in Surabaya by K.H. Hasyim Asy'ari and others. Traditionalist Sunni, following the Shafi'i school; estimated membership 60–90 million; champions Islam Nusantara and Indonesian pluralism. |
| Muhammadiyah | Indonesia's second-largest Islamic organization, founded 1912 in Yogyakarta by Ahmad Dahlan; modernist-reformist orientation emphasizing ijtihad (independent reasoning); manages thousands of schools, universities, and hospitals; estimated membership 29–35 million. |
| blasphemy law | Presidential Decree PNPS No. 1/1965 on the Prevention of Blasphemy and Abuse of Religions; prohibits "deviant interpretations" of recognized religions; maximum five-year imprisonment; upheld by Constitutional Court on April 19, 2010; instrument of prosecution of Ahmadiyah, minority Muslims, and the Jakarta governor Ahok. |
| Perda Syariah | Regional government bylaws (peraturan daerah) incorporating Islamic content, enacted by district and provincial governments following the decentralization laws of 1999; approximately 78 such regulations documented 1999–2007 by Robin Bush, covering Islamic observance, dress codes, and prohibition of alcohol and gambling. |
| penghayat | Penghayat kepercayaan; practitioners of indigenous, traditional, and syncretic belief systems outside the six state-recognized religions; estimated 12–20 million Indonesians; allowed to register beliefs on national ID cards following Constitutional Court Case No. 97/PUU-XIV/2016 (November 2017). |
| 212 movement | The series of mass demonstrations in Jakarta in November–December 2016, culminating in the December 2, 2016 rally at Monas (Aksi Bela Islam III, "Aksi 212"), organized primarily by FPI and allied Islamist groups to demand blasphemy prosecution of Jakarta Governor Ahok; one of the largest demonstrations in Indonesian history. |
| Jemaah Islamiyah | Al-Qaeda-linked jihadist network founded January 1, 1993 in Malaysia by Abdullah Sungkar and Abu Bakar Ba'asyir; responsible for the October 2002 Bali bombings (202 killed) and other attacks across Southeast Asia; formally dissolved by leadership June 30, 2024. |
| Jamaah Ansharut Daulah | Indonesian umbrella organization of at least 24 extremist groups pledging allegiance to the Islamic State (IS/ISIS), formed 2015 under ideological leader Oman Rochman; responsible for the January 2016 Jakarta attack and May 2018 Surabaya church bombings; banned by South Jakarta District Court July 2018. |
| Densus 88 | Detachment 88; elite National Police counterterrorism unit established 2003 following the Bali bombings with U.S. and Australian support; primary instrument of dismantling Jemaah Islamiyah's operational network; extended into deradicalization programming through the Rumah Wasathiyah initiative (2025). |
| Ahmadiyya | A Muslim religious movement founded in Punjab (British India) by Mirza Ghulam Ahmad in 1889; its theological position on prophethood is regarded by mainstream Sunni scholars as incompatible with Islamic orthodoxy; approximately 200,000–400,000 adherents in Indonesia; subject to 2008 Joint Ministerial Decree restricting activities and widespread mob violence. |
