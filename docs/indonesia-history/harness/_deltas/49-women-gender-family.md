## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Bundo Kanduang | Minangkabau concept of the "real mother" — a mature woman who holds ceremonial authority, custodianship of lineage property, and recognized communal voice; symbolizes the female centre of matrilineal society. |
| Dharma Wanita | New Order state organization (est. 1974) for wives of civil servants, ranked by their husbands' administrative grade; a key instrument of State Ibuism. |
| Gerwani | Gerakan Wanita Indonesia (Indonesian Women's Movement), founded 1950; the largest women's organization in the non-communist world by 1965; banned and destroyed after 1965, its members targeted by fabricated military propaganda. |
| Hari Ibu | National Mother's Day (December 22), established to commemorate the First Indonesian Women's Congress of 22–25 December 1928 in Yogyakarta. |
| jilbab | Indonesian term for the Islamic headscarf (hijab); banned under Suharto in state schools, permitted from c. 1991, increasingly mandatory in many post-Reformasi provincial contexts. |
| Kongres Perempuan Indonesia | Indonesian Women's Congress; first convened 22–25 December 1928 in Yogyakarta, establishing the national women's movement and the PPPI umbrella organization. |
| nyai | In the Dutch colonial East Indies, an Indonesian woman who served as the concubine and housekeeper of a European man; a figure of ambiguous status without legal protection. |
| PKK | Pemberdayaan dan Kesejahteraan Keluarga (Family Welfare Guidance); New Order nationwide program extending domestic ideology to rural women through village-level nutrition, childcare, and skills programs. |
| rantau | The Minangkabau tradition of young men leaving their matrilineal home village to seek education or fortune; in wider Indonesian usage, migration generally. |
| State Ibuism | Term coined by Julia Suryakusuma (1988 thesis) for the New Order's official gender ideology, which defined women primarily as wives and mothers in service to the state; institutionalized through Dharma Wanita and PKK. |
