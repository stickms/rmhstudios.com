## GLOSSARY
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| romusha | Forced/conscripted Indonesian labourers (overwhelmingly Javanese) mobilized by the Japanese occupation, 1942–45; hundreds of thousands died. |
| PETA | *Pembela Tanah Air*, "Defenders of the Homeland" — the Indonesian-officered auxiliary army the Japanese raised on Java from October 1943; cadre of the later republican army. |
| Putera | *Pusat Tenaga Rakyat*, "Center of the People's Power" — Japanese-sponsored nationalist mass organization (1943–44) led by the Empat Serangkai. |
| Jawa Hokokai | The Java Service Association (from January 1944), a tightly controlled occupation mass organization that replaced Putera. |
| Empat Serangkai | The "four-in-a-cluster" / nationalist "four-leaf clover" — the four Putera leaders: Sukarno, Hatta, Ki Hajar Dewantara, and Kyai Haji Mas Mansur. |
| BPUPK | *Badan Penyelidik Usaha Persiapan Kemerdekaan*, the Investigating Committee for the Preparation of Independence, convened on Java from late May 1945. |
| PPKI | *Panitia Persiapan Kemerdekaan Indonesia*, the Preparatory Committee for Indonesian Independence (from 7 August 1945), which adopted the 1945 constitution. |
| jugun ianfu | "Comfort women" — the euphemism for women and girls subjected to military sexual slavery in the Japanese-occupied Indies. |
| pemuda | The youth; the radicalized young Indonesians who became the driving force of the 1945 revolution. |
| gunseikan | Chief of the Japanese military administration in an occupied territory. |
| Kenpeitai | The Japanese military police, which ruled through arbitrary arrest, torture, and execution. |
