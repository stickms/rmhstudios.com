## GLOSSARY
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| abangan | In Clifford Geertz's framework, nominally Muslim Javanese whose religious life blends Islamic observance with animist and Hindu-Buddhist elements (contrast *santri*). |
| Dar al-Islam | "The abode of Islam" — the lands under Muslim rule; Ibn Battuta regarded Samudra Pasai as its easternmost edge in 1345. |
| Islamization | The long, uneven, regionally variable process by which Islam spread through the archipelago and was woven into local society, law, and culture. |
| madhhab | A school of Islamic law; the Shafi'i *madhhab*, dominant in Yemen and coastal India, prevailed in early Sumatran Islam. |
| priyayi | In Geertz's trichotomy, the Javanese aristocratic-bureaucratic elite whose culture drew on Hindu-Buddhist court traditions. |
| santri | Devout, observant Muslims in Geertz's framework (contrast *abangan*). |
| Serambi Mekah | "Veranda of Mecca" — Aceh's epithet as the pilgrims' embarkation point and a centre of Islamic scholarship. |
| suluk | Javanese Sufi devotional poetry fusing Islamic mysticism (*wahdat al-wujud*) with older Indic notions of pantheism and monism. |
| syahbandar | Harbour-master of a port city, often drawn from resident foreign merchant communities. |
| tarekat | A Sufi order or brotherhood (Arabic *tariqa*); attested in indigenous Indonesian sources mainly from the seventeenth century onward. |
| ulama | Muslim religious scholars; networks of *ulama* linked the archipelago to Mecca and Medina. |
| wahdat al-wujud | The Sufi doctrine of the "unity of being," central to Malay and Javanese mysticism and a recurring point of orthodox-reformist dispute. |
| Wali Songo | The "Nine Saints" credited by tradition with the Islamization of Java, remembered for spreading Islam through Javanese cultural forms. |
