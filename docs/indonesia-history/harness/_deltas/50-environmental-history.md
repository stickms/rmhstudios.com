## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| peatland | A wetland ecosystem in which partially decomposed plant matter (peat) accumulates over millennia under waterlogged, oxygen-poor conditions; Indonesian peatlands in Kalimantan and Sumatra store enormous quantities of carbon and, when drained, become highly susceptible to fire. |
| REDD+ | "Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation plus" — an international climate mechanism that compensates developing countries for maintaining or increasing their forest carbon stocks; Indonesia has been a major REDD+ recipient. |
| Wallace Line | The biogeographical boundary running through the Lombok Strait (between Bali and Lombok) and the Makassar Strait (between Borneo and Sulawesi), identified by Alfred Russel Wallace in the 1850s, separating the Asian-continental fauna to the west from the Australasian fauna to the east. |
| Wallacea | The biogeographic transition zone of central Indonesia (principally Sulawesi, Maluku, and the Lesser Sunda Islands east of Bali) that lies between the Sunda and Sahul continental shelves, never connected to either by dry land; noted for extreme endemism. (Defined here as complementing the Ch.1 keyterm for Wallacea; Ch.1 also defines it but this chapter expands the conservation and biodiversity dimension.) |
| Toba catastrophe theory | The hypothesis (Ambrose 1998) that the ~74,000 BP super-eruption of the Toba caldera in Sumatra caused a volcanic winter severe enough to reduce global human populations to a small founding group; the theory is contested by archaeological evidence (Petraglia et al. 2007 and subsequent studies) showing human continuity through the eruption event in India. |
| FOLU Net Sink | Indonesia's Forestry and Other Land Use Net Sink 2030 target, formalised under Presidential Regulation No. 98/2021, aiming for net negative greenhouse-gas emissions of 140 MtCO₂e from the land-use sector by 2030. |
| kolong | (Bangka-Belitung) The shallow ponds left in exhausted alluvial tin-mine workings; now a dominant landscape feature of the Bangka-Belitung islands after three centuries of extraction. |
| swidden | Shifting or rotational cultivation (also called ladang in Indonesian), in which a plot is cleared and cultivated for one or a few seasons before being left to regrow; under traditional management with adequate fallow periods, a sustainable and sophisticated agroforestry system used across the outer islands. |
