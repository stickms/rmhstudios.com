## GLOSSARY

New terms to append to `glossary-ledger.md` (keep alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Berkeley Mafia | Informal name for the U.S.-trained Indonesian economists (notably Widjojo Nitisastro, Ali Wardhana, Emil Salim) who served as Suharto's chief economic advisers from 1966, pursuing orthodox stabilisation and market-opening policies. |
| BIMAS | *Bimbingan Massal* (Mass Guidance) — the New Order's national rice intensification programme, distributing high-yielding seed varieties, subsidised fertiliser, pesticides, and supervised credit to farmers from the late 1960s. |
| BULOG | *Badan Urusan Logistik* (State Logistics Agency) — state body responsible for procuring, storing, and distributing strategic commodities, especially rice. Bought rice at a guaranteed floor price and sold to urban consumers at a subsidised ceiling price. |
| cukong | (from Hokkien for "boss") An ethnic-Chinese businessman who provides capital and managerial expertise to a politically connected military or government patron, receiving in return licences, concessions, and protection. Central to New Order political economy. |
| HPH | *Hak Pengusahaan Hutan* — a logging concession right granted by Indonesia's Ministry of Forestry over a defined forest area. Under the New Order, HPH concessions were allocated to politically connected firms, driving large-scale deforestation. |
| IPTN | *Industri Pesawat Terbang Nurtanio* (later Nusantara) — Indonesia's state aircraft manufacturer, established 1976 under B.J. Habibie's direction as part of the New Order's strategic-industries programme. |
| Pertamina | *Perusahaan Pertambangan Minyak dan Gas Bumi Negara* — the Indonesian state oil and gas company, created in 1968. Its near-collapse in 1975, with over US$10 billion in debt under Ibnu Sutowo, was the New Order's first major institutional crisis. |
| REPELITA | *Rencana Pembangunan Lima Tahun* (Five-Year Development Plan) — the series of six national development plans (1969–1998) that structured New Order economic policy. Prepared by Bappenas under Widjojo Nitisastro. |
| SPSI | *Serikat Pekerja Seluruh Indonesia* (All-Indonesia Workers' Union) — the sole legally recognised trade union federation under the New Order, integrated into the corporatist state apparatus. Independent unions such as SBSI (*Serikat Buruh Sejahtera Indonesia*) were suppressed. |
