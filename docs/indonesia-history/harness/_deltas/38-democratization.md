## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| AMM (Aceh Monitoring Mission) | Joint EU-ASEAN monitoring force of 300+ observers that oversaw implementation of the 2005 Helsinki MoU; mandate expired December 2006 following successful Aceh provincial elections. |
| DPD (Dewan Perwakilan Daerah) | Regional Representative Council, created by the 2002 constitutional amendment; 128 elected members (four per province); can submit legislation but cannot independently pass it — Indonesia's asymmetric upper chamber. |
| GAM (Gerakan Aceh Merdeka) | Free Aceh Movement; insurgent organization founded 4 December 1976 by Hasan di Tiro; fought for an independent Acehnese state; signed the 2005 Helsinki MoU and transformed into a political movement. |
| INTERFET | International Force East Timor; UN Security Council–authorized multinational force (UNSC Res. 1264) that entered East Timor on 20 September 1999 following post-referendum militia violence; commanded by Australian Maj. Gen. Peter Cosgrove; 22 contributing nations. |
| KPK (Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi) | Indonesia's Corruption Eradication Commission, established under Law No. 30/2002 and operational from late 2003; given powers exceeding ordinary prosecutors including warrantless wiretapping; achieved a 100% conviction rate in its early years. |
| pemekaran | The proliferation of new administrative districts through subdivision of existing ones, accelerated by Indonesia's 1999 decentralization laws; district numbers rose from 292 to 434+ by 2003. |
| politik uang | "Money politics" — the practice of vote-buying and patronage distribution at every level of Indonesia's electoral system, intensified by the open-list proportional representation system adopted under Reformasi. |
| POLRI (Kepolisian Negara Republik Indonesia) | Indonesian National Police; separated from the armed forces (TNI) by MPR Decree No. VI/2000 as part of Reformasi military reform. |
| Tipikor (Pengadilan Tindak Pidana Korupsi) | Indonesia's specialized anti-corruption court, created alongside the KPK, which hears KPK prosecutions exclusively; modeled on the Philippines' Sandiganbayan. |
| TNI (Tentara Nasional Indonesia) | Indonesian National Armed Forces; renamed from ABRI after the separation of POLRI (2000); subject to formal abolition of dwifungsi under Reformasi. |
| UNAMET | United Nations Mission in East Timor; administered the August 1999 independence referendum. |
