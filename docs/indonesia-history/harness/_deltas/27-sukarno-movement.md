## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Algemeene Studieclub | General Study Club, discussion group founded by Sukarno and colleagues in Bandung c.1925–26; its journal Soeloeh Indonesia Moeda published Sukarno's 1926 synthesis essay; reformed into the PNI in 1927. |
| Bahasa Indonesia | The standardised national language of Indonesia, declared the "language of unity" in the 1928 Sumpah Pemuda; based on an elevated and unified form of trade Malay (Melayu dagang), chosen over Javanese for its supraethnic, cross-archipelago reach. |
| Boven Digul | Dutch colonial internment camp in the malaria-infested jungles of western New Guinea (now Papua); used to confine PKI rebels after 1927 revolts and nationalist leaders (including Hatta and Sjahrir) from 1934–36. |
| Indonesia Menggugat | "Indonesia Accuses" — Sukarno's defence oration at his 1930 Bandung trial, a five-part indictment of imperialism drawing on Hobson, Lenin, and Luxemburg; one of the most widely circulated nationalist texts of the colonial era. |
| Indonesia Raya | "Great Indonesia" — the national anthem of Indonesia, composed by Wage Rudolf Supratman c.1924 and premiered on violin at the Second Youth Congress on 28 October 1928; renamed from "Indonesia" in 1929; banned by Dutch authorities; proclaimed the national anthem on 17 August 1945. |
| Marhaenisme | (Marhaenism) The political ideology developed by Sukarno, adapting Marxist class analysis to Indonesian social conditions. The marhaen — the impoverished small peasant owner — represented Indonesia's colonial masses, defined as the social base of the independence movement. |
| noncooperatie | Non-cooperation with the colonial government; the strategy adopted by the PNI and Perhimpoenan Indonesia of refusing to work within Dutch colonial institutions as a matter of nationalist principle. |
| Partai Nasional Indonesia (PNI) | Indonesian National Party, founded by Sukarno in Bandung on 4 July 1927; the first party to use "Indonesia" in its name and to demand full independence; dissolved 1931 after Sukarno's imprisonment. |
| Pendidikan Nasional Indonesia | Indonesian National Education (PNI-baru / New PNI); the cadre-education organisation led by Hatta and Sjahrir from 1931, emphasising intellectual preparation of democratic citizens over mass mobilisation. |
| Perhimpoenan Indonesia | Indonesian Union; the Indonesian students' association in the Netherlands, evolving from the Indische Vereeniging (1908) through the Indonesische Vereeniging (1922) to the Perhimpoenan Indonesia (1924); under Hatta's chairmanship pioneered use of "Indonesia" and "Indonesia Merdeka" as political slogans. |
| Soeloeh Indonesia Moeda | Torch of Young Indonesia; the journal of the Algemeene Studieclub in Bandung, in which Sukarno published his 1926 essay "Nationalism, Islam and Marxism." |
