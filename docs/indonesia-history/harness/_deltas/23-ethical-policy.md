## GLOSSARY

New terms to append to `glossary-ledger.md` (keep alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Agrarische Wet | The Agrarian Law of 1870 that opened the Dutch East Indies to private plantation capital; its *Domein Verklaring* (domain declaration) designated land without documented private ownership as state property available for long-term lease. |
| Cultuurstelsel | The "Cultivation System" introduced in 1830 by Governor-General van den Bosch, compelling Javanese villages to devote 20% of their land or 66 days of labour per year to colonial export crops; dismantled 1863–1870. |
| Cultuurgebied | The "cultivation belt" or plantation zone; specifically the east coast of Sumatra (Deli residency, modern North Sumatra), premier tobacco-growing region and the primary site of the coolie labour system after 1870. |
| Domein Verklaring | The "domain declaration" principle of the 1870 Agrarian Law: all land not demonstrably under documented private or communal use was deemed state domain, available for lease to European plantation companies. |
| Ethische Politiek | The "Ethical Policy" proclaimed by Queen Wilhelmina in 1901, committing the colonial state to three pillars — *irrigatie* (irrigation), *migratie* (transmigration), and *onderwijs* (education) — for indigenous welfare. |
| koeli / coolie | A colonial-era contract migrant labourer, typically Chinese or Javanese, recruited to work on Outer Island plantations under the Coolie Ordinance of 1880 and its penal sanction. |
| poenale sanctie | The "penal sanction" built into the Coolie Ordinance of 1880, making breach of a plantation labour contract a criminal (not merely civil) offence, enforceable with imprisonment or forced labour on public works. |
| transmigrasi | State-sponsored transmigration programme (begun 1905 under the Ethical Policy) relocating Javanese settlers to the less-densely populated Outer Islands; vastly expanded under the New Order. |
