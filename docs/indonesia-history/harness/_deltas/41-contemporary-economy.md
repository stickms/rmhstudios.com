## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| BPS | *Badan Pusat Statistik* — Statistics Indonesia, the national statistical agency responsible for economic, demographic, and social data collection, including the census and household surveys. |
| demographic dividend | The period in a country's development when the working-age population (roughly 15–64 years) is large relative to dependants, creating favourable conditions for accelerated economic growth if accompanied by productive employment, education, and health investment. Indonesia's dividend is expected to peak in the 2030s. |
| hilirisasi | Indonesian government policy of "downstreaming" — prohibiting or taxing exports of raw mineral commodities to force value-added processing to occur domestically, capturing industrial rents rather than exporting unprocessed ore. Most prominently applied to nickel (export ban from January 2020) and bauxite (from June 2023). |
| IKN | *Ibu Kota Nusantara* — the formal designation of Indonesia's new capital city project in East Kalimantan, authorised by UU No. 3/2022. IKN (also KIPP for the core government precinct) is commonly used as shorthand for the relocation project as a whole. |
| KPK | *Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi* — the Corruption Eradication Commission, an independent anti-corruption body founded in December 2003. Widely regarded as effective in its first decade; institutional independence weakened by Law 19/2019, which reclassified it as an executive-branch agency. |
| Nusantara (new capital) | The name given to Indonesia's new capital city under construction in Penajam Paser Utara, East Kalimantan. Distinct from the classical use of *Nusantara* as a Malay concept denoting the wider island world of the archipelago; the name deliberately invokes that classical resonance to signal national unity. |
| super app | A mobile application platform consolidating multiple services — ride-hailing, food delivery, digital payments, e-commerce, financial products — within a single user interface. Gojek's GoApp was Indonesia's pioneering example; the model suited markets where smartphone adoption outpaced legacy banking and retail infrastructure. |
