## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| abangan | Javanese Muslims whose practice incorporates syncretic Hindu-Buddhist and animist elements alongside Islam; distinguished from *santri* by colonial and later scholarly usage. |
| Budi Utomo | "Noble Endeavour"; Javanese cultural and social organisation founded at STOVIA, Batavia, on 20 May 1908; conventionally treated as the symbolic start of the national awakening. |
| *Ethische Politiek* (Ethical Policy) | Dutch colonial development programme proclaimed 1901, resting on the three pillars of irrigation, emigration, and education; nominally operative until 1942. Its expansion of Western schooling created the educated elite that challenged colonial rule. |
| *ijtihad* | Independent scholarly interpretation of Islamic scripture, as opposed to *taqlid* (unquestioning conformity to established legal rulings); central to Muhammadiyah's modernist Islamic vision. |
| Indische Partij | "Indies Party"; first political organisation in the Netherlands East Indies to declare independence as its explicit goal, founded 25 December 1912 by Douwes Dekker (Setiabudi), Tjipto Mangoenkoesoemo, and Soewardi Soerjaningrat (Ki Hajar Dewantara). Banned in 1913. |
| Muhammadiyah | Islamic modernist social and educational organisation, founded 1912 in Yogyakarta by Ahmad Dahlan; advocates *ijtihad* and condemns syncretic practice; built a nationwide network of schools and clinics. |
| Nahdlatul Ulama | "Renaissance of the Scholars"; traditionalist Islamic organisation founded 31 January 1926 in East Java by Hasyim Asy'ari; defends classical Ash'ari theology, the Shafi'i legal school, and the *pesantren* network. |
| *pergerakan* | "The movement"; the collective term for the Indonesian nationalist movement of the early twentieth century, encompassing its political, social, and cultural organisations. |
| *pesantren* | Traditional Islamic boarding school in which students (*santri*) live with and study under a senior Islamic scholar (*kyai*); the organisational and social base of Nahdlatul Ulama. |
| *priyayi* | The Javanese aristocratic and administrative class occupying the lower and middle rungs of the colonial civil service; the seedbed of early nationalism and also, for many, a stratum tied to the colonial order. |
| santri | Devout Indonesian Muslims committed to Islamic orthodoxy and education in the *pesantren* tradition; as a sociological category contrasted with *abangan* in analyses of Javanese religious culture. |
| Sarekat Islam | "Islamic Union"; originally Sarekat Dagang Islam (1911), renamed 1912; under Tjokroaminoto it became the colony's first mass movement, claiming two million members by 1919 before fracturing over communist–Islamic tensions in the early 1920s. |
| *taqlid* | Unquestioning conformity to established Islamic legal rulings, as opposed to *ijtihad*; defended by traditionalist scholars and opposed by modernists such as Ahmad Dahlan. |
