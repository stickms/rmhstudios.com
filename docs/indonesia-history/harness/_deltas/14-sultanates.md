## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Age of Commerce | Anthony Reid's term for the period c. 1450–1680 in Southeast Asia, characterized by booming maritime trade, urban growth, Islamic and Christian conversion, gunpowder technology, and the dominance of trade-based city-states. |
| adipati | Javanese title for a regional governor or duke appointed by the central court; Sultan Agung installed <i>adipati</i> across Mataram's expanded realm. |
| Adat Aceh | The legal code of the Sultanate of Aceh, systematized under Iskandar Muda, blending customary (<i>adat</i>) and Islamic jurisprudence. |
| extirpatie | Dutch VOC policy of systematically destroying clove and nutmeg trees on non-designated islands to enforce spice monopoly; devastated Maluku island communities. |
| Kejawen | The syncretic spiritual and cultural tradition of Central Javanese court society, blending pre-Islamic Hindu-Buddhist concepts of divine kingship with Islamic piety; characteristic of the Mataram court. |
| karaeng | Makassarese noble or chiefly title, roughly equivalent to Javanese <i>adipati</i>; became a marker of Makassarese diaspora identity after the Makassar War. |
| orang kaya | Literally "rich person" in Malay; the mercantile and noble elite of port-city sultanates such as Banten, Makassar, and the Maluku spice islands; a key intermediary between sultan and trading population. |
| Panembahan | Javanese honorific title below "Sultan," meaning "lord" or "revered one"; used by Senapati before the Mataram rulers adopted the full title Sultan. |
| Wali Songo | "Nine Guardians" or "Nine Saints"; the council of Islamic scholars traditionally credited with converting Java to Islam in the late fifteenth and early sixteenth centuries; associated with the Demak Sultanate. |
| ulama | Arabic plural of <i>ʿalim</i>; Islamic religious scholars trained in law, theology, and Quranic interpretation; played formal administrative roles (as <i>qadi</i> and <i>mufti</i>) at the Demak court and were central to Aceh's scholarly culture. |
