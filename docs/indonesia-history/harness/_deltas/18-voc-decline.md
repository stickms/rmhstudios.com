## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| hongi-tochten | Annual VOC extirpation expeditions in the Moluccas using kora-kora war canoes to destroy clove trees and punish trade with non-Dutch merchants; name from the Ternatan word for "armada." |
| kora-kora | Maluku war canoes used in the annual hongi extirpation expeditions; crewed by conscripted Ambonese rowers under corvée obligation. |
| orang kaya | Literally "rich men" in Malay: the merchant-aristocratic leaders of Bandanese (and other Maluku) society who governed interisland trade and negotiated (and resisted) with the VOC. |
| perk | The equal-sized plantation unit into which the VOC divided the Banda Islands after 1621; sixty-eight perken were established across Lontor, Ay, and Banda Neira by 1628. |
| perkenier | The manager of a perk; typically a former VOC employee assigned an estate and twenty-five enslaved workers, obligated to sell all produce to the VOC at company-set prices. |
| Vergaan Onder Corruptie | "Perished under corruption": a popular eighteenth-century inversion of the VOC's initials satirising the systematic embezzlement, nepotism, and illegal private trading that characterised the company in its final decades. |
| pecinan | Chinatown district; in Batavia, the area outside the city walls (present-day Glodok, Jakarta) to which all surviving ethnic Chinese were confined after the 1740 massacre. |
| Heeren XVII | The "Lords Seventeen": the board of directors governing the VOC from Amsterdam, drawn from the major investing chambers (particularly Amsterdam). |
| Treaty of Bongaya | The 1667 agreement between Cornelis Speelman and Sultan Hasanuddin of Gowa (Makassar) ending the VOC-Gowa war; thirty articles establishing Dutch monopoly, vassal status for Gowa, and Fort Rotterdam. |
