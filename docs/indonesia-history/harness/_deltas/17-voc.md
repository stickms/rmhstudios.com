## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Batavia | Fortified Dutch colonial city founded 30 May 1619 by Jan Pieterszoon Coen on the ruins of Jayakarta; served as VOC's Asian headquarters until 1942; renamed Jakarta under Japanese occupation. |
| bewindhebbers | The managing directors of the VOC (60–76 in total), drawn from each chamber; the Heeren XVII were elected from among them. |
| cartaz | A sea-pass system used by the Portuguese *Estado da India*: Indian Ocean traders paid Portugal for a license to trade without interference; fees provided over 85% of the viceroy's revenue by the 1580s. |
| company-state | Scholarly term (associated with Adam Clulow, Arthur Weststeijn, Philip J. Stern) for early modern chartered companies — especially the VOC and EIC — that exercised quasi-sovereign powers (war, treaty-making, coinage, capital punishment) in their territories, constituting an "alternative sovereign formation" alongside the emerging nation-state. |
| Estado da India | The Portuguese "State of India" (c. 1505–1961): a Crown-administered maritime empire in Asia, funded by cartaz fees and customs, that the VOC displaced from most of the Indonesian archipelago. |
| Heeren XVII | See *Heren XVII* (alternate Dutch spelling). |
| Heren XVII | The Lords Seventeen; central governing board of the VOC, drawn from the *bewindhebbers* of the six chambers. Amsterdam held 8 of 17 seats, Zeeland 4, smaller chambers 1 each (17th rotated). Met 2–3 times per year. |
| intra-Asian trade | The VOC's network of trade routes connecting Asian markets with one another (e.g., Bengal textiles exchanged for Japanese silver), distinct from the Europe-Asia *carriera* trade; a major source of VOC profits and commercial innovation. |
| Jayakarta | Javanese port town (also written Jacatra; a vassal of Bantam) on the Ciliwung River that Coen burned in May 1619 to found Batavia. |
| participanten | Ordinary shareholders in the VOC, as distinct from the *bewindhebbers* (managing directors); liable only to their paid-in capital. |
| perkeniers | Dutch planters who received parcels (*perken*) of Banda Islands nutmeg gardens after the 1621 conquest, in exchange for selling output exclusively to the VOC at fixed low prices. |
| voorcompagnieën | The competing Dutch pre-companies that sent ~15 fleets to Asia between 1595 and 1601, whose ruinous price competition prompted the States General to charter the VOC as a unified monopoly. |
