## GLOSSARY
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| bendahara | The chief minister or grand vizier of a Malay sultanate, combining administrative, judicial, and military authority; at Malacca the most powerful officer of state after the sultan (e.g., Tun Perak). |
| daulat | The sacral, quasi-supernatural sovereign power believed to inhere in a legitimate Malay ruler; its counterpart was *derhaka* (treason), making loyalty a cosmic as well as a political duty. |
| derhaka | Treason or rebellion against a legitimate Malay ruler, conceived as a violation of the cosmic order that *daulat* would itself punish. |
| entrepôt | A port that thrives by gathering, warehousing, and redistributing the goods of others rather than producing its own; Malacca was the classic Southeast Asian example. |
| orang laut | "Sea people," the boat-dwelling maritime communities of the Straits and Riau; their loyalty furnished Srivijaya and Malacca with naval power and the means to channel trade to the royal port. |
| Sejarah Melayu | The "Malay Annals," a court chronicle of the Malacca sultanate compiled at Johor in the early 17th century; a masterpiece of classical Malay literature but a legitimising, genealogical text to be read critically. |
| shahbandar | A harbourmaster in Indian-Ocean and Southeast-Asian ports; at Malacca a college of four shahbandars each managed a group of foreign merchant nationalities, allotting warehouses and settling disputes. |
