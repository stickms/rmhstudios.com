## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Pergerakan | Literally "the movement"; the broad Indonesian nationalist awakening of the early twentieth century, encompassing Islamic, communist, and secular-nationalist tendencies within a shared anti-colonial project. |
| Santri | A pious Muslim, often one educated in a pesantren (Islamic boarding school); in the sociological literature on Indonesian Islam, used for the devout Muslim segment of Javanese society, contrasted with the more nominally Muslim abangan. |
| PKI (Partai Komunis Indonesia) | The Communist Party of Indonesia, constituted at Semarang in May 1920 from the Dutch colonial socialist group ISDV. Widely considered the first communist party established in Asia, destroyed as a legal organization after the 1926–27 uprisings and again after 1965. |
| Nahdlatul Ulama (NU) | "The Rising of the Scholars," a traditionalist Islamic mass organization founded in Surabaya on 31 January 1926 under Kyai Haji Hasyim Asy'ari. Defends classical Sunni legal traditions and pesantren-based Islamic practice. |
| Muhammadiyah | A reformist Islamic mass organization founded in Yogyakarta on 18 November 1912 by Kyai Haji Ahmad Dahlan. Promotes scripturally purified Islamic practice and runs a nationwide network of schools, hospitals, and social welfare institutions. |
| Marhaenisme | Sukarno's adaptation of Marxist class analysis to Indonesian conditions, centered on the marhaen (small peasant or artisan structurally impoverished by colonialism). Allowed Sukarno to deploy Marxist analysis while constructing a specifically Indonesian revolutionary subject. |
| PNI (Partai Nasional Indonesia) | The Indonesian National Party, founded in Bandung on 4 July 1927 by Sukarno. The first Indonesian political organization to declare full independence as its explicit and immediate goal. |
| Boven Digul | Dutch colonial internment camp established 1927 on the Digul River, southwestern Dutch New Guinea (now Papua, Indonesia); held approximately 1,300 political prisoners following the 1926–27 uprisings; a major instrument of colonial political repression through 1942. |
| Pan-Islamism | The political idea that the world's Muslim communities share a common identity and should act in solidarity, especially against imperial powers; a key reference point in the ideological debates of the early Indonesian nationalist movement. |
| ISDV (Indische Sociaal-Democratische Vereeniging) | Indies Social-Democratic Association, founded by Sneevliet in Surabaya in May 1914; precursor of the PKI. Used the "bloc within" tactic to radicalize Sarekat Islam from the inside. |
| Discipline clause (disiplin partai) | The rule adopted by the Fifth Central Sarekat Islam Congress in October 1921 requiring SI members to hold no other party membership, effectively expelling PKI members from the central organization. A pivotal rupture in Indonesian political history. |
