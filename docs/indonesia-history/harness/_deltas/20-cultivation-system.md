## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Cultuurstelsel | Dutch: "Cultivation System"; Indonesian: tanam paksa ("forced planting"). The colonial agricultural-taxation regime introduced in Java in 1830 under Governor-General Johannes van den Bosch, requiring villages to devote 20% of their land (or 66 days of annual labour) to government-specified export crops in lieu of a cash land tax. |
| batig slot | Dutch: "favourable balance." The annual surplus of colonial revenues over colonial expenditures remitted from the Dutch East Indies to the Dutch treasury. Between 1831 and 1877 the cumulative batig slot totalled approximately 823 million guilders. |
| cultuurprocenten | Dutch: "cultivation percentages." Commission payments made to Javanese priyayi officials under the Cultivation System, proportional to the volume of government crops they extracted. Structured to incentivise overextraction. |
| priyayi | The hereditary Javanese administrative nobility serving as intermediaries between the Dutch colonial state and Javanese peasantry. Under the Cultuurstelsel they held formal authority as regents (bupati), district heads (wedana), and village chiefs (lurah). |
| ereschuld | Dutch: "debt of honour." Concept advanced by Conrad van Deventer (1899) arguing the Netherlands owed the Indies compensation for wealth extracted through the batig slot. Underpinned the Ethical Policy of 1901. |
| Multatuli | Pen name of Eduard Douwes Dekker (1820–1887), from Latin multa tuli ("I have suffered much"). Author of Max Havelaar (1860). |
| NHM (Nederlandsche Handel-Maatschappij) | Netherlands Trading Company, a Dutch crown enterprise established 1824 and granted a monopoly on the shipping and sale of Javanese produce in Europe until 1872. Key mechanism for transferring colonial surplus to the Dutch economy. |
| Suikerwet | Dutch: Sugar Law (1870). Legislation phasing the Dutch colonial government out of forced sugar cultivation between 1878 and 1890. Part of the transition to the Liberal Period. |
| Agrarische Wet | Dutch: Agrarian Law (1870). Introduced domein verklaring (state ownership of "unclaimed" land) and permitted private European entrepreneurs to lease colonial land for up to 75 years, opening the Liberal Period of private plantation enterprise. |
| domein verklaring | Dutch: "domain declaration." Clause in the 1870 Agrarian Law declaring all land not demonstrably under adat (customary) occupation to be state property, available for private lease. Dispossessed many village communities of communally used forests and upland fields. |
| tanam paksa | Indonesian term for the Cultuurstelsel: "forced planting." Standard Indonesian historiographical designation for the system. |
