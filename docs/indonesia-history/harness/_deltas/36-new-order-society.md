## GLOSSARY

New terms to merge into glossary-ledger.md (keep alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| BKKBN | *Badan Koordinasi Keluarga Berencana Nasional* — the National Family Planning Coordinating Board, established 1970, which administered Indonesia's Keluarga Berencana programme. |
| cukong | Javanese/Indonesian term for a Chinese-Indonesian businessman acting as financier and commercial partner to a military officer or government official; the structural relationship that made the New Order simultaneously exclude and depend on Chinese capital. |
| dangdut | Indonesia's most popular contemporary music genre, crystallized in the early 1970s from Malay *orkes melayu*, North Indian film music, and rock; associated with urban migrant workers and increasingly Islamicised through the career of Rhoma Irama. |
| Dharma Wanita | "Women's duty/service" — the New Order's obligatory organization for wives of male civil servants, established 1974; its internal hierarchy mirrored the civil-service rank of members' husbands, embodying the "appendage status" at the core of State Ibuism. |
| Dwifungsi | Already in ledger. |
| ICMI | *Ikatan Cendekiawan Muslim se-Indonesia* — the All-Indonesia Association of Muslim Intellectuals, founded 7 December 1990 under B.J. Habibie's chairmanship with Suharto's endorsement; the main organisational vehicle of the late New Order's accommodation of political Islam. |
| Keluarga Berencana (KB) | Indonesia's national family planning programme, administered by BKKBN; promoted the two-child ideal (*Dua Anak Cukup*, "Two is Enough") and achieved one of the fastest fertility transitions in the developing world, reducing the total fertility rate from approximately 5.6 (early 1970s) to 2.6 (mid-1990s). |
| pembangunan | "Development" — the New Order's central ideological concept, which transformed a contested policy goal into an unquestionable national telos, as analysed by Ariel Heryanto. |
| Pers Pancasila | The official doctrine governing all Indonesian print and broadcast media under the New Order, requiring reporting to subordinate objectivity to national unity and development goals; also called "developmental journalism." |
| PKK | *Pembinaan Kesejahteraan Keluarga* — Family Welfare Guidance; a village-level women's organization that served as the primary community delivery vehicle for state programmes including KB contraception, nutrition, and Pancasila ideological education. |
| Posyandu | *Pos Pelayanan Terpadu* — integrated village health post; the community delivery point for KB contraception, maternal health, immunization, and nutrition monitoring, staffed by volunteer women trained through PKK. |
| State Ibuism (*Ibuisme Negara*) | Term coined by Julia Suryakusuma (1996) for the New Order's official gender ideology, which constructed Indonesian womanhood around domesticity, reproductive compliance, and subordination — women as mothers and wives first, citizens second — institutionalized through Dharma Wanita and PKK. |
| TVRI | *Televisi Republik Indonesia* — the state television network, established 1962, which held a monopoly on broadcast television until 1989; operated under Ministry of Information editorial guidelines aligned with New Order developmental ideology. |
