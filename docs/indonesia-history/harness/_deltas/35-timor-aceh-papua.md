## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Fretilin | *Frente Revolucionária de Timor-Leste Independente* — the principal independence movement of East Timor, founded 1974; led the short-lived 1975 provisional government and the resistance throughout Indonesian occupation. |
| GAM (*Gerakan Aceh Merdeka*) | The Free Aceh Movement, founded 4 December 1976 by Hasan Muhammad di Tiro; fought Indonesian forces intermittently from 1976 until the 2005 Helsinki peace agreement. |
| DOM (*Daerah Operasi Militer*) | "Military Operations Zone" — a legal designation giving the Indonesian military extraordinary counterinsurgency powers and suspending civilian oversight, applied to Aceh 1989–1998. |
| CAVR (*Comissão de Acolhimento, Verdade e Reconciliação*) | Commission for Reception, Truth and Reconciliation in East Timor; its 2005 *Chega!* report estimated at least 102,800 conflict-related deaths during the occupation (1974–1999). |
| Falintil | *Forças Armadas de Libertação Nacional de Timor-Leste* — the armed wing of the East Timorese resistance, originally Fretilin's military branch; led by Xanana Gusmão from the early 1980s. |
| OPM (*Organisasi Papua Merdeka*) | Free Papua Movement, founded in the early 1960s; the armed resistance organisation opposing Indonesian rule in West Papua/Irian Jaya. |
| PEPERA (*Penentuan Pendapat Rakyat*) | "Determination of the People's Opinion" — Indonesia's official name for the 1969 "Act of Free Choice" in West Papua; widely criticised as illegitimate since only 1,025 hand-picked representatives voted. |
| Operasi Seroja | "Operation Lotus" — the Indonesian military's codename for the December 1975 invasion of East Timor; the largest military operation in Indonesian history to that date. |
| UNTEA | United Nations Temporary Executive Authority — the UN body that administered West New Guinea from October 1962 to 1 May 1963 before transferring it to Indonesia under the New York Agreement. |
| transmigration (*transmigrasi*) | Indonesian government programme, greatly expanded under the New Order, relocating settlers from Java and Bali to outer islands including Papua; produced significant demographic change in receiving provinces. |
