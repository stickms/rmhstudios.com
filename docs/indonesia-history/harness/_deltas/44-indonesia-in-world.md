## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| bebas-aktif | Indonesia's foundational foreign policy doctrine meaning "free and active": non-alignment with great-power blocs combined with proactive international engagement; first articulated by Vice-President Hatta in 1948. |
| ASEAN centrality | The principle that ASEAN and its associated frameworks should be the primary platforms through which great powers engage Southeast Asia, preventing any single power from dominating the region. |
| nine-dash line | China's claimed maritime boundary in the South China Sea, encompassing approximately 90 percent of that sea; found incompatible with UNCLOS by the Permanent Court of Arbitration in 2016. |
| Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) | President Xi Jinping's global infrastructure investment strategy (launched 2013), under which China funds large-scale transportation, energy, and manufacturing projects in partner countries, often through state-backed loans. |
| hilirisasi | Indonesian term for "downstreaming" — the policy of banning raw mineral exports to force foreign investors to establish domestic processing industries, notably applied to nickel ore from 2020. |
| Melanesian Spearhead Group (MSG) | Intergovernmental organization of four Melanesian states (Fiji, PNG, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu) plus New Caledonia's FLNKS; formalized 2007. Indonesia joined as associate member in 2015. |
| Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) | International forum founded in Belgrade in 1961, grouping states that declined alignment with either Cold War superpower bloc; grew directly from the 1955 Bandung Conference. |
| BRICS | Originally Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa (joined 2010); expanded at 2023 Johannesburg summit; Indonesia joined as full member January 2025, becoming first Southeast Asian BRICS member. |
| multi-alignment | Contemporary term for a foreign policy strategy that deepens relationships with multiple great powers simultaneously without committing to any single alliance or bloc; Indonesia's approach under Jokowi and Prabowo. |
| IJEPA | Indonesia-Japan Economic Partnership Agreement (signed 2007, in force 2008) — bilateral free-trade and investment framework covering goods, services, energy, intellectual property, and customs cooperation. |
