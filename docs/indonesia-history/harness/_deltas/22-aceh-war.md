## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| *perang sabil* | Literally "war in the path of God"; a form of defensive jihad against non-Muslim invaders; the religious framing adopted by Acehnese ulama from c. 1881 to sustain resistance against Dutch colonial conquest. |
| uleebalang (*ulèëbalang*) | Hereditary Acehnese adat chiefs holding territorial authority at village and district level; their power derived from customary law and land control rather than Islamic learning; distinguished by Snouck Hurgronje from the ulama as potentially co-optable by the Dutch. |
| *Hikayat Perang Sabil* | "Ballad of the Holy War"; Acehnese-language text composed by Teuku Chik Pante Kulu, deployed by ulama as a mobilizing recitation in village prayer houses (*meunasah*) throughout the Aceh War; promised paradise to fighters and damnation to collaborators. |
| *meunasah* | Acehnese village prayer house, serving as community gathering place, Islamic school, and (during the Aceh War) site for the nightly recitation of the *Hikayat Perang Sabil*. |
| Korps Marechaussee te voet | Elite Royal Netherlands East Indies Army mobile infantry corps, founded 2 April 1890; armed with carbine-length Mannlicher rifle and *klewang* short sword; used offensive surprise tactics in jungle counterinsurgency in Aceh. |
| *puputan* | A Balinese concept of ritual mass death in battle: a final charge to certain death by a ruler and court against an overwhelming enemy, preserving martial honor rather than submitting to capture; notable instances at Badung (1906) and Klungkung (1908). |
| *Korte Verklaring* | "Short Declaration"; standardized Dutch submission treaty imposed on Indonesian rulers from the 1890s onward requiring unconditional acknowledgment of Dutch sovereignty; the principal legal instrument by which the Netherlands East Indies was juridically unified. |
| *rencong* | Traditional Acehnese dagger; the weapon used in *Atjèh-moord* suicide attacks against Dutch soldiers. |
| *Atjèh-moord* | Dutch term for individual Acehnese suicide attacks against Dutch soldiers, religiously motivated by the *perang sabil* tradition; a distinctive and deeply unsettling feature of the guerrilla phase of the war. |
