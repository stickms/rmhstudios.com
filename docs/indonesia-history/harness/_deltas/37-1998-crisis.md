## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| ABRI | *Angkatan Bersenjata Republik Indonesia* — the Armed Forces of the Republic of Indonesia under the New Order; renamed TNI (*Tentara Nasional Indonesia*) in 1999 after Reformasi reforms removed the police. |
| BLBI | *Bank Indonesia Liquidity Support* — emergency central-bank liquidity injections after the 1997 bank closures; became one of Indonesia's largest financial fraud scandals as funds were diverted by bank owners. |
| DPR | *Dewan Perwakilan Rakyat* — the People's Representative Council, Indonesia's lower house of parliament. |
| Golkar | *Golongan Karya* ("Functional Groups") — the New Order state party through which Suharto's government controlled the political system; consistently won supermajority elections from 1971 to 1997. |
| Hari Kebangkitan Nasional | "National Awakening Day," celebrated on 20 May, marking the 1908 founding of Budi Utomo; the May 20, 1998 demonstrations used its symbolism deliberately. |
| KKN | Abbreviation of *korupsi, kolusi, nepotisme* ("corruption, collusion, nepotism") — the central slogan of the 1997–98 student movement and the defining critique of Suharto-era governance. |
| Komnas HAM | *Komisi Nasional Hak Asasi Manusia* — the National Commission on Human Rights, established 1993; investigated both the Trisakti shootings and the May 1998 riots. |
| Komnas Perempuan | National Commission on Violence Against Women; established by Presidential Decree No. 181/1998 directly in response to the documented sexual violence of May 1998. |
| Kostrad | *Komando Cadangan Strategis Angkatan Darat* — Army Strategic Reserve Command; led in May 1998 by Lieutenant General Prabowo Subianto. |
| mahasiswa | Indonesian: university student(s). In political discourse, refers to the organised student movement that has historically intervened at national crises (1966, 1974, 1978, 1998). |
| MPR | *Majelis Permusyawaratan Rakyat* — the People's Consultative Assembly, the supreme legislative body under the 1945 Constitution; elected the president before direct elections began in 2004. |
| NKK/BKK | *Normalisasi Kehidupan Kampus/Badan Koordinasi Kemahasiswaan* — Suharto's 1978–79 regulations banning autonomous student political organisations on campuses, driving organising underground. |
| Orde Baru | Indonesian: "New Order." The authoritarian regime Suharto established after 1966, lasting until his resignation on 21 May 1998. |
| TGPF | *Tim Gabungan Pencari Fakta* (Joint Fact-Finding Team) — established by President Habibie on 23 July 1998 to investigate the May 1998 riots; released its Final Report on 23 October 1998 documenting widespread violence including sexual violence against ethnic-Chinese women. |
| Tionghoa | The preferred Indonesian term for ethnic Chinese Indonesians in the post-1998 era; replaced the derogatory colonial-era term *Cina* in official and scholarly usage after Reformasi. |
