## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| abangan | Nominally Muslim Javanese whose practice blended Islam with Hindu-Buddhist and animist traditions; one pole of Clifford Geertz's influential framework for understanding Indonesian social cleavages. |
| Benteng programme | Indonesia's first affirmative-action economic initiative (early 1950s), reserving import licences and preferential credit for indigenous (*pribumi*) entrepreneurs to displace Dutch and Chinese commercial dominance; terminated as a failure by the late 1950s. |
| Jakarta Charter (*Piagam Jakarta*) | A document drafted by Indonesian nationalist leaders on 22 June 1945 that included the obligation for Muslim Indonesians to follow Islamic law; the "seven words" were removed from the final 1945 Constitution on 18 August 1945 and became the central contested issue in the Konstituante debates (1956–1959). |
| Konstituante | The Constituent Assembly elected in December 1955 (520 seats) to draft a permanent constitution; deadlocked over the basis-of-state question (Pancasila vs. Islam) and dissolved by Sukarno's presidential decree of 5 July 1959. |
| Masyumi | *Majelis Syuro Muslimin Indonesia* — Council of Indonesian Muslim Associations; the major modernist Islamic political party of the parliamentary era, strong in the outer islands and urban educated Muslim communities. |
| non-alignment | Foreign-policy orientation refusing alignment with either Cold War bloc; advocated by Sukarno and institutionalized at the Bandung Conference (1955) and the founding of the Non-Aligned Movement (Belgrade, 1961). |
| PRRI-Permesta | Two overlapping regional rebellions (1958): *Pemerintah Revolusioner Republik Indonesia* (Sumatra) and *Piagam Perjuangan Semesta Alam* (Sulawesi); demanding structural political-fiscal reform rather than secession; suppressed by 1961. |
| RIS (*Republik Indonesia Serikat*) | Republic of the United States of Indonesia: the federal state created by the 1949 Round Table Conference settlement, composed of sixteen constituent states; dissolved into the unitary Republic on 17 August 1950. |
| santri | Consciously and observantly Muslim Indonesians oriented toward Islamic law and scholarship; one pole of Clifford Geertz's abangan/santri cleavage framework for Javanese society. |
| SOB (*Staat van Oorlog en Beleg*) | State of war and siege, declared by Sukarno in March 1957 after the collapse of the Ali II cabinet; effectively ended parliamentary governance. |
| UUDS 1950 | *Undang-Undang Dasar Sementara* — Provisional Constitution of 1950, governing Indonesia 1950–1959; established parliamentary cabinet government, extensive civil rights, and an independent judiciary, pending a permanent constitution. |
