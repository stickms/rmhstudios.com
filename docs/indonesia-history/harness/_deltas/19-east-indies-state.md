## GLOSSARY

New terms for glossary-ledger.md (keep alphabetical):

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| bupati | The Javanese "regent" who headed a regency (*kabupaten*) as the highest rank in the Pangreh Praja indigenous colonial bureaucracy; typically hereditary in practice; served as intermediary between Dutch resident and Javanese population. |
| Binnenlands Bestuur | "Interior Administration" — the Dutch colonial civil service that operated alongside the indigenous Pangreh Praja; staffed by European officials from resident down to controller. |
| gelijkstelling | "Equalisation" — the legal mechanism by which an indigenous person in the Dutch East Indies could petition to be reclassified under European law, typically granted on grounds of Christian conversion or adoption of European lifestyle. |
| landrente | The land-rent or land-tax system introduced by Raffles (1813) and retained in modified form by the Dutch; levied a cash proportion of annual crop value on Javanese cultivators in lieu of forced deliveries or labour obligations. |
| Pangreh Praja | "Ruler of the Realm" — the indigenous Javanese civil service hierarchically structured as bupati (regent, c. 70), wedana (district chief, c. 400), and asisten wedana (sub-district chief, c. 1,200); operated under Dutch Binnenlands Bestuur supervision. |
| priyayi | The Javanese aristocratic class that staffed the Pangreh Praja and served as intermediaries between Dutch colonial administrators and the Javanese population; identity defined by noble descent and government service. |
| Regeringsreglement | "Government Regulation" — the fundamental constitutional document of the Netherlands East Indies; first issued 1818 by the Commissioners-General, comprehensively revised 1854 to codify the three-tier racial classification of the colonial population. |
| residentie | Residency — the basic territorial unit of Dutch colonial administration, each headed by a European resident responsible to the Governor-General; established by Daendels (1808) and retained throughout the colonial period. |
