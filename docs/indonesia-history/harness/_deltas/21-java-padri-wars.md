## GLOSSARY

New terms to append to `harness/glossary-ledger.md` (terms not already in the ledger):

**benteng stelsel** — Dutch: "fortress system." Counterinsurgency strategy adopted by General Hendrik Merkus de Kock from 1827 during the Java War: a dense network of small fortified posts positioned close enough for mutual support, linked by roads, with mobile columns operating between them to gradually deny guerrillas territory, supply, and civilian cover. Applied subsequently in Aceh and other colonial campaigns.

**Cultuurstelsel** — Dutch: "Cultivation System." Compulsory cash-crop cultivation regime introduced on Java in 1830 by Governor-General Johannes van den Bosch to recoup the financial losses of the Java War and the Belgian secession. Required peasant households to dedicate one-fifth of their arable land (or 66 days of labour per year) to designated export crops for the colonial state. Operated c. 1830–1870; generated massive profits for the Dutch treasury while causing famine and hardship on Java.

**KNIL** — abbreviation of *Koninklijk Nederlands Indisch Leger* (Royal Netherlands Indies Army), the professional colonial military force of the Netherlands East Indies, established by royal decree in September 1814 and dissolved in 1950. Comprised European officers, European contract soldiers, and indigenous recruits; Ambonese and Menadonese soldiers from Christian eastern Indonesia became increasingly prominent and valued during the nineteenth century.

**Padri** — name for the Wahhabi-influenced Islamic reformist movement in the Minangkabau highlands of West Sumatra, active c. 1803–1837, and for its members. Etymology disputed: derived either from *Pedir/Pidie* (Acehnese port of embarkation for Mecca) or from Portuguese *padre* (priest/father, adopted into Malay). Padri adherents sought to impose strict Sharia and eliminate matrilineal *adat* customs.

**perang sabil** — from Arabic *sabilillah* ("in the path of God"): a declaration of holy war in the Islamic tradition, framing armed conflict as religious duty and promising spiritual reward for fighters and martyrs. Diponegoro declared *perang sabil* against the Dutch in 1825; the concept recurred in Acehnese and other Indonesian resistance movements through the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

**ratu adil** — Javanese: "Just King/Ruler." Messianic concept rooted in prophecies attributed to the twelfth-century Kediri king Jayabaya, predicting a divinely chosen leader who would expel foreign oppressors and restore justice and prosperity. The belief recurred in periods of social upheaval; Diponegoro was widely identified as the *ratu adil* by his followers during the Java War.

**Babad Diponegoro** — Javanese autobiographical chronicle dictated by Prince Diponegoro during exile in Manado, 1831–1832; c. 1,150 pages in Javanese verse (pegon script). The most important primary source for the Java War from the Javanese perspective; inscribed as a UNESCO Memory of the World document in 2013.

**nagari** — Minangkabau term for the village republic, the fundamental political and social unit of Minangkabau society; governed by *adat* (customary law) through councils of *penghulu* (clan chiefs). The conflict between Padri reformers and the *nagari* adat system was the internal dimension of the Padri War.
