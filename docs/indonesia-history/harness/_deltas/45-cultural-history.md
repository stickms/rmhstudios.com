## GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| batik | Wax-resist textile dyeing technique applied to cotton or silk, in which hot wax drawn with a canting pen resists dye; UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage since 2009. Central to Javanese ceremonial life from birth to death. |
| candi | Hindu-Buddhist temple monument of Java; term derives from the death goddess Candika (an aspect of Durga). The great examples (Borobudur, Prambanan) date to the 8th–9th centuries CE. |
| dalang | The master puppeteer and sole performer of a wayang kulit performance, responsible for voicing all characters, narrating, cuing the gamelan, and sustaining performances from dusk to dawn. |
| gamelan | Traditional Indonesian bronze percussion orchestra, consisting primarily of gongs, metallophones, and drums, supplemented by flute, fiddle, and zither. UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage since 2021. |
| ikat | Textile technique in which yarn bundles are bound and resist-dyed before weaving, creating patterns in the finished cloth; includes warp-ikat, weft-ikat, and the extremely rare double-ikat (geringsing, from Tenganan Pegringsingan, Bali). |
| joglo | Traditional Javanese aristocratic house form, defined by four central pillars (soko guru) supporting a soaring inner roof (brunjung); interior organised from public veranda (pendapa) through pringgitan to private dalem. |
| kakawin | Long narrative poems composed in Old Javanese (Kawi) using Sanskrit-derived meters (kavya prosody), written at the courts of Java and Bali from approximately the 9th to the 16th centuries. |
| rumah gadang | The traditional communal matrilineal longhouse of the Minangkabau people of West Sumatra; owned by the women of the clan, with characteristic multi-gabled buffalo-horn roof. |
| tajug | The multi-tiered pyramidal timber roof form of traditional Javanese and archipelago mosque architecture, derived from Hindu-Buddhist meru-tower precedent; exemplified by the Demak Great Mosque (c. 1477–79). |
| tongkonan | The traditional Toraja clan house of central Sulawesi, raised on piles with a dramatically pitched saddle roof whose upswept gables evoke a boat or buffalo; functions as residence, clan hall, and ancestral shrine. |
| undha-usuk | The Javanese speech-level system encoding social hierarchy in vocabulary choice; principal registers are ngoko (informal/low) and krama (formal/high), with honorific (krama inggil) and humble (krama andhap) sub-registers. |
| Balai Pustaka | The Dutch colonial government's official publishing house (founded 1917), simultaneously a censorship mechanism and the institutional incubator of modern Indonesian prose fiction. |
| Angkatan '45 | The "Generation of '45," the cohort of Indonesian writers who emerged around independence, characterised by free verse, individualism, and rejection of Balai Pustaka conventions; led by Chairil Anwar. |
| hikayat | Classical Malay prose narrative genre encompassing heroic romances, royal chronicles, and miracle tales; pre-eminent literary form of the Malay-Islamic maritime world. |
| syair | Classical Malay narrative verse form in four-line rhyming quatrains; used for devotional, romance, and historical purposes from at least the 16th century (Hamzah Fansuri). |
