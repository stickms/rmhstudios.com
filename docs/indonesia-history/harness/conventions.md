# Conventions — *Indonesia: A Comprehensive History*

**Append-only.** Every chapter author reads this file before writing and appends any new
spelling/style decision here. Consistency across ~50 chapters depends on it.

## Preferred spellings (use these exactly)

- **Srivijaya** (not Sriwijaya, Srividjaja)
- **Sailendra** (the Central Javanese dynasty; not Shailendra)
- **Majapahit** · **Singhasari** (not Singosari) · **Kediri** · **Tarumanagara** · **Kutai**
- **Mataram** — disambiguate as *(Hindu-Buddhist) Mataram* vs *(Islamic) Mataram Sultanate*
- **Borobudur** · **Prambanan** · **Trowulan** (Majapahit capital site)
- **Sukarno** (not Soekarno; note the spelling "Soekarno" appears in primary sources — gloss once)
- **Suharto** (not Soeharto; same primary-source note)
- **Yogyakarta** (informal: Yogya) · **Surakarta** (Solo) · **Batavia** (colonial Jakarta) → **Jakarta** (1942+)
- **the Dutch East Indies** / **Netherlands East Indies** (the colony) vs **VOC** (the company, 1602–1799)
- **Pancasila** · **Konfrontasi** · **Reformasi** · **pribumi** · **adat** · **kraton** · **wayang** (italicize foreign terms on first use)
- **Nusantara** — use for (a) the classical archipelago concept and (b) the new capital; disambiguate.
- Ethnic groups: **Javanese, Sundanese, Balinese, Minangkabau, Buginese, Acehnese, Batak, Dayak, Papuans**

## Dates & periods

- Use **CE / BCE**. Approximate dates: **c.** (e.g., *c. 800 CE*). Centuries spelled out: *the seventh century*.
- Reconcile every date you assert against `master-timeline.md`; if it conflicts, resolve and note it.
- Period/Part names (use verbatim as section `part` labels):
  Part I — Land, Peoples, Deep Time · Part II — Classical Hindu-Buddhist Era · Part III — Islam & the Sultanates ·
  Part IV — Encounter & the VOC · Part V — The Colonial State · Part VI — National Awakening ·
  Part VII — Occupation, Revolution & Sukarno · Part VIII — The New Order ·
  Part IX — Reformasi & Contemporary Indonesia · Part X — Thematic Syntheses

## Citations (Chicago notes-bibliography)

- In prose, mark a citation with `<sup class="cite">N</sup>` where N is the chapter-local note number.
- At chapter end: `<h2 id="chN-notes" class="notes-h">Notes</h2>` then `<ol class="endnotes"><li>…</li></ol>`.
  The Nth `<li>` corresponds to `<sup class="cite">N</sup>`.
- Endnote format (first mention): `Author First Last, *Title* (Place: Publisher, Year), p. N.`
  Web source: `Author/Org, "Page Title," Site, accessed Month Year, URL.`
- Every source must also be appended to `bibliography.md`.

## Figures

- Number figures `Figure <chapter>.<n>` (e.g., *Figure 9.2*). Caption every figure.
- Real images: `<figure class="photo">`; caption ends with a `<span class="credit">` carrying source URL, creator, license.
- Diagrams: Mermaid `<figure class="mermaid-fig">`. No raw `<` / `>` inside Mermaid labels.
