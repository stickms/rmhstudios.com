## Looking Ahead

Ten months ago, Slice It! was a prototype — a single-player rhythm game with rough beatmap generation and a half-finished UI. Today, it's a real platform with multiplayer, a community song library, thousands of plays, and an identity that's uniquely its own.

But we're far from finished. If anything, the foundation we've built has only expanded the canvas of what's possible. I want to share where Slice It! is heading — the features we're actively developing, the ideas we're prototyping, and the long-term vision that keeps us motivated.

This isn't a polished marketing roadmap. This is an honest look at what's on the workbench. Some of these features will ship exactly as described. Some will evolve through playtesting. Some might be cut entirely if they don't work in practice. That's game development.

## Ranked Competitive Mode

The most requested feature since launch. Players want a structured competitive system with visible stakes — not just leaderboards, but a **ranking system** that reflects skill over time.

Here's what we're designing:

### Seasonal Ranks

Players will be placed into ranked tiers based on their competitive performance across a season (roughly 3 months). We're considering a tiered system: **Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, and Maestro.** Each tier has subdivisions, and players advance or drop based on match results.

### Ranked Match Format

A ranked match will work as a best-of-three format between two players. Songs are selected through a **pick-ban system** — both players see a pool of 5 songs, each bans one, and they alternate picks for the remaining three. This adds strategic depth beyond raw timing skill. Knowing your opponent's weaknesses, choosing songs that favor your strengths, and banning songs you struggle with all become part of the game.

### Anti-Smurf Measures

New accounts will go through **placement matches** (10 games) before receiving their initial rank. We'll also monitor for statistically improbable performance patterns that suggest a skilled player deliberately deranking.

## Custom Beatmap Editor

The automatic beatmap generator is Slice It!'s signature feature, and it's gotten remarkably good. But it's not perfect — and for some songs, a human mapper can create a more satisfying experience.

We're building a **browser-based beatmap editor** that lets advanced users create and share hand-crafted maps. The editor will feature:

- **Timeline view** with waveform visualization and a scrollable note placement interface
- **Snap-to-beat guides** that help mappers place notes on rhythmically consistent positions
- **Live preview** — play through your map in real time while editing, adjusting notes on the fly
- **Import from auto-gen** — start with the automatically generated beatmap and refine it, rather than starting from scratch
- **Community sharing** — hand-crafted maps will appear alongside auto-generated ones in the song library, tagged as "community maps"

The auto-gen maps aren't going anywhere. The editor is an addition, not a replacement. The philosophy remains: any song should be instantly playable without waiting for someone to manually chart it. But for players who want to craft the definitive map for their favorite song, the tools should exist.

## New Game Modes

The standard "notes scroll toward a hit zone" format is the core of Slice It!, but we're experimenting with alternative modes that offer different experiences:

### Zen Mode

No score. No misses. No failure state. Notes flow and you tap along. Any input registers as a hit. This is for pure relaxation — listening to music and engaging with it physically without any pressure. Background visuals respond to your inputs, creating a personalized light show synchronized to the music.

### Endurance Mode

A continuous stream of songs with no breaks. Your health bar depletes on misses and regenerates on hits. How long can you survive? The difficulty gradually ramps up over time, starting at Chill and escalating toward Expert. Endurance runs are tracked on a separate leaderboard ranked by total time survived.

### Speed Mode

The normal beatmap, but the scroll speed increases over the course of the song. By the final chorus, notes are flying at double the normal speed. It tests a completely different skill set — not just timing, but rapid visual processing and adaptability.

### Tag Team Mode

A cooperative multiplayer mode where two players share the same note highway. Notes are color-coded — blue notes are Player 1's responsibility, pink notes are Player 2's. Miss a note, and your partner's screen flashes as a warning. Your combined score is what counts. It's rhythm gaming as a trust exercise.

## Improved Audio Analysis

The beatmap generator is our core technology, and it's under continuous improvement. Key areas of active research:

### Genre-Aware Generation

Different genres have different rhythmic conventions. An EDM track and a jazz piece have fundamentally different structures, and the beatmap generator should understand that. We're training genre classification models that detect the style of a song and adjust generation parameters accordingly — onset sensitivity, note density curves, pattern preferences.

### Vocal Isolation

Using spectral processing techniques inspired by tools like LALAL.ai, we're experimenting with isolating vocal lines from the mix and using them to generate dedicated note lanes. Imagine a lane that follows the melody while other lanes track the percussion and bass. This creates maps that feel like you're playing *every part* of the song.

### BPM and Downbeat Detection

While we intentionally don't rely on BPM metadata, knowing the song's tempo and where the downbeats fall improves map quality significantly. We're implementing a BPM detection algorithm that analyzes the autocorrelation of the onset detection signal to find the most likely tempo and phase. This information helps the post-processor align notes to rhythmically sensible positions.

## Social Features

Music is social, and Slice It! should be too. Beyond the existing multiplayer system and community library, we're planning:

### Player Profiles

Public profiles showing a player's stats, favorite songs, recent activity, and competitive rank. Other players can follow profiles to see when friends are online or when they set a new high score.

### Clubs

Groups of players who share songs, compete in club leaderboards, and organize multiplayer sessions. Think of them as Discord servers within Slice It! — each with their own song playlists, chat, and weekly challenges.

### Replay Sharing

After finishing a song, players can save and share a replay. The replay file stores input data and the beatmap, allowing anyone to watch exactly how a top score was achieved. This serves both a social function (showing off) and an educational one (learning from better players).

## Mobile Support

This is a big one and one of our most ambitious goals. Slice It! currently works on desktop browsers with keyboard input. But rhythm games are fantastic on mobile — touch input is natural and satisfying.

The challenges are significant:

- **Touch input mapping** — redesigning the note highway for taps and swipes rather than key presses
- **Performance** — maintaining smooth rendering on mobile GPUs and browsers
- **Audio latency** — mobile browsers have notoriously high audio latency, making calibration even more critical
- **Responsive design** — the neumorphic UI needs to work beautifully on small screens

We're prototyping a mobile-optimized game mode with a simplified layout — four lanes across the bottom of the screen that you tap directly. Early tests are promising.

## The Long-Term Vision

When I step back and think about where I want Slice It! to be in two years, the vision is clear:

**Slice It! should be the place where music and play converge.** A platform where any song is instantly playable, where players compete and connect in meaningful ways, where a thriving community of music lovers shares discoveries and celebrates great gameplay.

Not a niche rhythm game for hardcore players. Not a casual toy that people try once and forget. A living, growing ecosystem that sits at the intersection of music, competition, and community.

The technical foundation is solid. The community is growing. The design identity is distinct and beloved. Now it's about execution — shipping features, listening to feedback, iterating relentlessly, and never losing sight of the thing that makes Slice It! special: the feeling of pressing a key at exactly the right moment, hearing the note ring out, and knowing *you* are part of the music.

Thank you for following along on this journey. Whether you've been here since the first devlog or you're just discovering Slice It! now, I'm grateful for your time and your attention. The best is still ahead.
