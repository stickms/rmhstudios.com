## Where We Are Now

It's been nine months since I shared the first concept post for Project Vega. In that time, we've built the core gameplay loop (pun intended), implemented four tower types, created a dynamic A* pathfinding system, designed a three-resource economy, and wrapped it all in the Clinical Glitch aesthetic. The foundation is solid, and I'm genuinely proud of what we've accomplished.

But foundations are just the beginning. Today I want to pull back the curtain on where Project Vega is headed — the features we're planning, the systems we're expanding, and the vision for what this game will become.

## New Tower Types

Four tower types create the strategic core, and I still believe that constraint serves the game well during the initial experience. But as players master the basics, they deserve new tools to explore. Here's what's on the workbench:

### The Fragmenter (Planned)

**Role:** Area-of-effect damage dealer
**Concept:** Fires a slow projectile that detonates at the target location, dealing damage to all enemies in a radius. The detonation leaves a lingering "memory fragment" zone that damages enemies who walk through it for 3 seconds.

The Fragmenter fills a gap in the current roster — we have single-target (Lobotomizer), rapid-fire (Synapse), utility (Suppressor, Echo), but no dedicated AOE. The lingering zone mechanic adds another dimension to positional planning, especially interesting as a Ghost Protocol.

### The Recursive (Concept Stage)

**Role:** Self-replicating support tower
**Concept:** Every 30 seconds, the Recursive tower creates a weaker copy of itself on an adjacent empty cell. These copies can create their own copies, spreading like a neural network across the grid.

This tower type leans into the mental health metaphor — it represents the brain building new neural pathways. Mechanically, it's a slow-burn investment: weak initially but exponentially powerful over time. As a Ghost Protocol across loops, a single Loop 1 Recursive could generate an entire network by Loop 3.

*I'm still working out the balance. Exponential growth in a tower defense game is either transcendent or broken, and the line between those is razor-thin.*

### The Dampener (Concept Stage)

**Role:** Anti-elite specialist
**Concept:** Does no damage to regular enemies. Instead, it applies a debuff to elite and boss enemies that reduces their stats by a percentage. Multiple Dampeners stack, and the debuff persists even after the enemy leaves range.

The Dampener is designed for late-game focused play. In early loops, it's nearly useless. But against the scaled-up elites of Loop 3's final wave, a few well-placed Dampeners can be the difference between survival and wipe.

## Boss Enemies

Currently, Vega's "boss waves" are just enhanced versions of existing enemy types. The roadmap includes **dedicated boss enemies** with unique mechanics:

### The Repressor

A massive entity that **disables Ghost Protocol towers** in its vicinity. Within a 4-tile radius of the Repressor, ghost towers stop firing. They resume once it passes, but during those seconds, your defense has a gaping hole.

This enemy type directly attacks the loop system's core promise — that your past investments are safe. The Repressor forces players to ensure they have active (non-ghost) towers capable of handling the gap, creating a new layer of defensive redundancy planning.

### The Intrusive Loop

An enemy that **reverses time locally**. When it enters a corridor, all towers in that corridor temporarily revert to their Level 1 stats, regardless of upgrades. The reversion lasts for 10 seconds — enough to create a terrifying window of vulnerability.

The Intrusive Loop embodies intrusive thoughts: sudden, unwanted disruptions to your carefully built mental frameworks. Fighting it isn't about raw firepower — it's about building defenses robust enough to function even at reduced capacity.

### The Amnesiac

An enemy that **erases one random Ghost Protocol tower** permanently when it reaches the Memory Core (in addition to Sanity damage). Losing a carefully placed Loop 1 Synapse ghost in the middle of Loop 3 is catastrophic. The Amnesiac makes Sanity damage even more punishing and incentivizes perfect defense.

## Expanded Loop Mechanics

### Four-Loop Mode

Once players master the three-loop structure, we'll introduce a **four-loop challenge mode**. Four loops of the same timeline means four layers of Ghost Protocols, more Neuroplasticity to spend, and enemy scaling that pushes even veteran players to their limits. The UI challenges alone are significant — visually distinguishing four different ghost tiers requires expanding our color palette.

### Branching Timelines

This is the most ambitious planned feature. Instead of three sequential loops of the same timeline, **branching timelines** would allow players to choose between two different enemy wave compositions for each loop. Loop 1 might branch into Timeline A (heavy Sprite focus) or Timeline B (heavy Hulk focus). Your choice determines which ghost configuration carries forward.

The strategic implications are wild. Do you choose the timeline you can handle best in the current loop, or the one that creates the most useful Ghost Protocols for future loops? This adds a strategic decision layer *before* the tower defense gameplay even begins.

### Loop Modifiers

Random modifiers applied at the start of each loop that change the rules:

- **Temporal Surge:** Loop plays at 1.5x speed. All tower fire rates increase proportionally.
- **Memory Fog:** Ghost Protocol towers are invisible until enemies enter their range.
- **Neurological Storm:** Random grid cells become temporarily blocked throughout the loop.
- **Hyperfocus:** One random tower type deals double damage this loop.

Modifiers inject variety into repeated play and prevent the meta from becoming stale. They're also great for content creation and competitive play — "how do you handle a Memory Fog + Hyperfocus(Suppressor) run?"

## Narrative Progression

Vega currently has no explicit narrative. The theme tells a story through mechanics and aesthetics, but there's no plot, no characters, no dialogue. I want to change that.

### The Operator

The planned narrative introduces **The Operator** — a voice that speaks to you through terminal text between loops. The Operator is running the clinical system you're interacting with. They provide context, guidance, and gradually reveal the nature of the Memory Core you're defending.

The narrative unfolds across multiple runs. Complete a run, and you unlock a new terminal log. The logs piece together a story about a patient undergoing an experimental memory therapy procedure. The "game" you're playing is the therapy interface. The enemies are intrusive neural patterns. The towers are cognitive behavioral interventions.

The meta-narrative recontextualizes the entire game without changing any mechanics. Everything you've been doing has always been this — you just didn't have the context to understand it.

### Terminal Logs

Between loops, optional terminal logs will appear on the inter-loop screen:

```
> OPERATOR LOG 003
> Patient response to Loop 2 intervention: POSITIVE
> Ghost Protocol integration: 87% stable
> Sanity metrics trending upward
> Note: Patient is developing novel defensive strategies
> not present in standard protocol library.
> This is... unexpected. Continue monitoring.
```

These logs are optional reading — players who want pure gameplay can skip them. But for those who engage, they add an emotional layer that makes the loop system resonate on a narrative level as well as a mechanical one.

## Technical Roadmap

### Performance Optimization

Current performance is solid on modern hardware, but I want Vega running smoothly on lower-end devices and older browsers. The post-processing pipeline needs a WebGL pathway for GPU-accelerated effects. Canvas pixel manipulation is the bottleneck, and offloading scanlines and chromatic aberration to fragment shaders could cut post-processing time by an order of magnitude.

### Mobile Adaptation

Tower defense is a natural fit for touch interfaces. The grid-based placement translates directly to tap-to-build. The main challenges are screen real estate (the UI needs significant rework for small screens) and performance (mobile GPUs are less forgiving with post-processing).

### Accessibility

Vega's visual effects — scanlines, glitches, chromatic aberration — are core to the aesthetic but potentially problematic for players with photosensitivity, epilepsy, or visual processing disorders. We're building a comprehensive accessibility settings panel:

- Toggle scanlines on/off
- Reduce or disable glitch effects
- Adjustable chromatic aberration intensity
- High-contrast mode with simplified visual effects
- Screen reader support for tower placement and enemy positions

Accessibility isn't optional. If Vega is about mental health, it needs to be playable by everyone.

## The Vision

Nine months ago, I asked a question: *what if your past mistakes could become your greatest advantage?* The answer became Project Vega — a game about fighting your own patterns, learning from repetition, and building something stronger each time through.

The core of that vision hasn't changed. But it's grown. What started as a mechanical hook has become a game with thematic depth, strategic richness, and a growing narrative ambition. Every feature on this roadmap serves that central question.

The loops continue. The ghosts persist. And the future of Project Vega is something I'm endlessly excited to build.

Stay tuned. The next loop is just beginning.
