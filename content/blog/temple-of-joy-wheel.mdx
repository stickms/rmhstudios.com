## The Prestige Problem

Every idle game has a prestige system. It's the moment where you sacrifice your progress, get some permanent currency, and start over stronger. Cookie Clicker has Heavenly Chips. Adventure Capitalist has Angel Investors. Universal Paperclips has… well, Universal Paperclips does something else entirely, but you get the idea.

Prestige is the genre's most important mechanic and its most misunderstood one. Done well, it transforms a linear number-go-up into a spiral — each loop tighter, faster, and more informed than the last. Done poorly, it feels like the game deleting your save file and asking you to be grateful about it.

I've played a lot of idle games where I hit the prestige button, watched my numbers reset, and immediately thought: *why did I do that?* The permanent bonuses felt abstract. The early game I'd already played through once was exactly as tedious the second time. The prestige currency bought upgrades that said "+10% income" in a way that made my eyes glaze over. I'd lost everything for a percentage point.

Temple of Joy's prestige system — the **Wheel of Samsara** — is my attempt to solve these problems. The solution, it turns out, was hiding in Buddhist philosophy the whole time.

## Samsara: The Cycle of Rebirth

In Buddhism, Samsara is the cycle of death and rebirth. Beings cycle through existences, carrying karma from one life to the next. The goal, in classical Buddhism, is to escape the cycle entirely — to achieve Nirvana and step off the wheel. But in Temple of Joy's interpretation, the wheel itself is the point. Each turn of the wheel makes you wiser. Each rebirth starts stronger. You're not trying to *escape* the cycle. You're trying to *perfect* it.

**Transcendence** is the act of turning the wheel. When your run happiness reaches a threshold (1e13 × 8^prestigeCount — exponentially increasing), you can choose to transcend. You lose your happiness, your sources, most of your upgrades, and your karma (unless you've purchased the Karmic Vessel). In return, you earn **Bliss Shards** — the premium currency that buys upgrades on the Wheel of Samsara.

The threshold curve is steep and deliberate. Your first transcendence requires 10 trillion happiness. Your second requires 80 trillion. Your fifth requires 327 billion trillion. Each transcendence demands that you push significantly further than the last, which means each run needs to be meaningfully more efficient — not just 10% faster, but fundamentally different in strategy and capability. The Wheel is what makes that possible.

## Ten Tiers of Rebirth

The Wheel of Samsara has ten tiers, each containing four to nine upgrades that permanently alter the rules of the game. This is what separates it from a generic prestige shop. These aren't percentage-based stat bumps. They're **game-changers** — each tier reshapes how you play.

### Tier I — The First Steps

Your first wheel purchases solve the immediate pain of prestige: *why does starting over suck?*

**Ember of Memory** lets you keep five upgrades across transcendence. You pick which five. This single upgrade transforms the early game from a tedious replay into a strategic puzzle — which five upgrades give you the fastest start? **Karmic Vessel** lets karma persist through transcendence, meaning your relics stay relevant. **Remembered Joy** gives you a ×5 HPS buff for the first 60 seconds of a new run — a burst of momentum that makes the first minute feel exciting instead of empty.

These are intentionally conservative. Tier I says: *prestige still hurts, but now it hurts less.* The player isn't powerful yet. They're just slightly less vulnerable.

### Tier II — Deepening

Tier II introduces the mechanic that makes me proudest of the whole system: **Samsara's Gift**. It grants +5% HPS per prestige, stacking up to 20 times. This means your 20th run has a permanent, unconditional +100% HPS bonus on top of everything else. It's a simple, visible, compounding reward for engaging with the prestige cycle. Every transcendence leaves a permanent mark.

**Deep Roots** starts your first five sources at quantity 5 instead of 0 — you immediately have passive income on a new run. **The Living Temple** grants +1 karma per click, turning your clicking into a karma-generation strategy for the first time. **Reincarnated Wealthier** starts you with 1% of your peak happiness from the previous run, giving late-game players a non-trivial starting bankroll.

Tier II is where the game starts feeling like a proper incremental loop. You're still grinding, but the grind has *texture*. Each run starts differently than the last.

### Tier III — Enlightenment

This is the tier that most radically changes gameplay. **Divine Memory** lets you keep *all* upgrades through transcendence. Not five. All of them. This means your upgrade tree — meticulously built over hours — persists forever. Prestige now only resets your sources, currencies, and milestones. The psychological impact is enormous. Transcendence stops feeling like a loss and starts feeling like a *launch*.

**Transcendence Momentum** gives ×3 HPS for the first three minutes of a new run. Combined with Remembered Joy's ×5 for the first minute, your post-transcendence opening is *explosive*. **The Lazy Pilgrim** introduces the first auto-buy tier — buying one source every 30 seconds. Automation begins.

**Prophet's Memory** (keep 20 upgrades) and **Enlightened Clicker** (HPC scales with prestige count) round out the tier. By this point, the player has transcended at least eight or nine times, and each transcendence takes less than an hour. The cycle is fast, the rewards are clear, and the compounding is intoxicating.

### Tiers IV–V — The Infinite

**Nirvana's Blueprint** starts you with 50% of one minute's worth of your peak HPS as initial happiness. At high prestige counts, your peak HPS is astronomical, so this gives you a *massive* starting bankroll. Paired with **Temple Eternal** (×10 baseline HPS), your first seconds post-transcendence now accumulate more happiness than your entire first run ever produced.

**The Remembering** at Tier V makes milestones persist. Milestones are flat HPS bonuses and multipliers earned at happiness thresholds, and in the base game they reset each transcendence, requiring you to re-earn them. With The Remembering, they stick. This is a huge quality-of-life upgrade that also serves a design purpose: it means players never have to slog through early milestones again, because those milestones were designed for the first-run experience.

**Karmic Overflow** (×10 karma, +2 relic slots) and **Bliss Overdrive** (×100 HPS for 5 minutes at run start) complete the mid-game prestige arc. The player is now a seasoned transcender. They've seen the cycle twenty-plus times. Each turn of the wheel is a victory lap.

### Tiers VI–X — The Endgame

The later tiers shift from solving problems to amplifying power. **Singularity Engine** (×50 HPS), **Nirvana Engine** (×500 HPS), **Dimensional Rift** (×5,000 HPS), and the final **Grand Design** (×100,000 HPS) stack into multipliers so large that they make the early game's numbers feel like rounding errors.

But the real endgame upgrades are about completeness. **Prophecy Complete** at Tier VIII lets you keep *all* upgrades AND offerings through transcendence — your source-specific multipliers, painstakingly purchased with karma, now persist forever. **Omega Memory** at Tier X keeps *everything* — sources, upgrades, offerings, milestones. At that point, transcendence isn't a reset at all. It's a button that gives you Bliss Shards.

And then there's **The Last Smile** — the final upgrade on the final tier, costing 2,000,000 Bliss Shards. It does two things: ×1,000,000 HPS (yes, one million), and each click generates one minute of HPS income. At that point, clicking isn't just a vestigial early-game mechanic anymore. It's the most powerful action in the game. The first thing you did — click the smile button — becomes the last thing that matters. I'm extremely pleased with that narrative closure.

## The Design Philosophy

Three principles guided the Wheel's design:

### 1. Each tier should change how you play, not just how fast you play.

Percentage multipliers are boring. "×2 HPS" is necessary filler, but it should never be the *headline* of a tier. The headline should be a rule change: "you keep your upgrades now." "Automation begins." "Milestones persist." "Clicking is powerful again." Each tier should make the player rethink their strategy, not just execute the same strategy 10% faster.

### 2. Early tiers should reduce pain; late tiers should amplify joy.

The first three tiers are about making prestige *not suck*. The last three tiers are about making prestige *feel incredible*. This mirrors the psychological arc of any repeated process: first you tolerate it, then you accept it, then you look forward to it. By Tier X, players *want* to transcend. The button feels like a celebration, not a sacrifice.

### 3. The cost curve should demand engagement, not just time.

Bliss Shard costs escalate from 1 (Beginner's Bliss) to 2,000,000 (The Last Smile). But the shard yield also scales: base 10 shards per prestige, +10 per prestige count, with Eternal Return (+25%), The Infinite Wheel (+15%), and The Infinite Wheel II (×2) compounding on top. A player's 50th transcendence yields hundreds of times more shards than their first. This means the late-game tiers aren't locked behind "transcend 10,000 times." They're locked behind "transcend efficiently, push further, optimize harder." The Wheel rewards *better play*, not just more time.

## The Narrative Arc of Rebirth

There's something happening in the Wheel that I didn't fully plan — it emerged from the mechanics. The ten tiers tell a story:

**Tiers I–III:** You are fragile. Each rebirth strips you bare, and you slowly learn to carry more of yourself through the transition. This is the novice phase — you're a soul being reborn, retaining echoes of past lives.

**Tiers IV–VI:** You are powerful. Your rebirths are fast and purposeful. You've mastered the cycle. You carry your wisdom, your tools, your accumulated karma. You are a practiced monk in the temple.

**Tiers VII–IX:** You are transcendent. The cycle barely touches you. Your multipliers are so vast that starting over takes minutes instead of hours. You've learned everything the game can teach.

**Tier X:** You are complete. The Omega tier. Prestige costs nothing. Your first click equals a minute of cosmic income. The cycle isn't a cycle anymore — it's a statement. You've turned the Wheel of Samsara so many times that the Wheel itself bends to you.

And then the game asks, one more time: *is this enough?*

## What I'd Change

If I rebuilt the Wheel from scratch, I'd adjust two things. First, I'd add more visual ceremony to transcendence — right now it's a confirmation dialog, but it should feel like a *moment*. A breath. An animation of the wheel turning. Something to mark the transition between lives.

Second, I'd make the shard yield more nuanced. Right now it's a formula. I'd love to introduce bonus shards for achieving specific goals during a run — "transcend with 100+ of any source," "transcend with all synergies active," "transcend without buying any indulgence upgrades." Turn each run into a challenge with optional objectives. The cycle already feels good. It could feel *purposeful*.

But that's for a future patch. For now, the Wheel turns, and each turn brings something new. Samsara isn't a punishment. It's a practice. And like all practices, it rewards the patient.
