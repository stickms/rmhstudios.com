# Grid vs. Reality

One of the greatest challenges in the *Echoes* 2D refactor was enemy navigation. In a world full of static obstacles and dynamic players, how do you make enemies feel smart?

## The Challenges
- **Node Costs**: Standard A* works well for static maps, but enemies need to react to player movement in real-time.
- **Corner Cutting**: Enemies often get stuck on the "corners" of obstacles. We solved this by expanding the collision grid by one cell.
- **Wall Sliding**: When A* fails (due to path depth limits), we implemented a vector-projection fallback that lets enemies "slide" along walls toward the player.

## Optimization
We staggered the pathfinding updates across enemies to prevent CPU spikes. Instead of every enemy calculating a path every frame, they update their intent every 0.5 seconds, using linear interpolation to fill the gaps.

Better navigation means more pressure on the player—and a better game.
