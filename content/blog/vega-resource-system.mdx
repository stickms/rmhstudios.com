## Why Three Resources?

Most tower defense games have one resource: gold. You earn it, you spend it on towers, rinse and repeat. It works, but it reduces every decision to a single axis — is this tower worth the gold cost?

For Project Vega, I wanted resource decisions to feel **multi-dimensional**. Every tower placement should involve weighing at least two competing concerns. Every loop transition should force players to make meaningful trade-offs. One resource can't do that. Two gets closer. Three is the sweet spot.

Meet Vega's resource trinity: **Focus**, **Sanity**, and **Neuroplasticity**. Each operates on a different timescale, serves a different purpose, and creates a different kind of tension.

## Focus: The Moment-to-Moment Currency

Focus is Vega's equivalent of gold. You earn it by killing enemies and spend it on building and upgrading towers. It's the tactical resource — the one you're constantly watching during active gameplay.

### Earning Focus

```typescript
interface FocusReward {
  anxietySprite: 5,
  depressionHulk: 25,
  manicSpike: 15,
  eliteVariant: (base: number) => base * 2.5,
}
```

Focus income scales with enemy difficulty. Anxiety Sprites give 5 Focus each — individually trivial, but swarms of them add up. Depression Hulks yield 25, rewarding players who invest in Lobotomizers to take them down. Manic Spikes give 15, a middle ground that rewards mastering their erratic patterns.

### Spending Focus

| Tower     | Build Cost | Upgrade (L2) | Upgrade (L3) |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Synapse   | 30        | 45           | 80           |
| Suppressor| 40        | 60           | 100          |
| Lobotomizer| 60       | 90           | 150          |
| Echo      | 50        | 75           | 120          |

The pricing reflects each tower's role. Synapses are cheap and spammable. Lobotomizers are premium investments. Echoes are mid-priced but gain disproportionate value from positioning — a well-placed Echo is worth far more than its Focus cost.

### Why Focus Works

Focus creates the classic TD tension: *do I build now or save for something better?* But in Vega, this tension is amplified by the loop system. Focus spent in Loop 1 creates Ghost Protocols that persist forever. Every Focus investment in early loops has compounding returns. This makes early-loop Focus feel incredibly precious — each point spent is an investment in your entire run.

## Sanity: The Stakes

Sanity is Vega's life system, but it's deliberately more anxious than a simple HP bar. You start each run at **100% Sanity**. Every enemy that reaches the Memory Core reduces Sanity by a different amount based on enemy type. When Sanity hits 0%, the run is over.

### Sanity Damage Table

| Enemy Type     | Sanity Damage |
|---------------|--------------|
| Anxiety Sprite | 2%           |
| Depression Hulk| 12%          |
| Manic Spike    | 6%           |

Here's the crucial part: **Sanity does not reset between loops**. If you end Loop 1 at 72% Sanity, Loop 2 begins at 72%. This makes Sanity a persistent pressure that accumulates across your entire run.

### The Sanity-Visual Feedback Loop

As Sanity drops, Vega's CRT post-processing effects intensify. Scanlines deepen. Chromatic aberration separates. Glitches become more frequent. The game is literally harder to *see* as your defenses fail.

```typescript
const getEffectIntensity = (sanity: number) => ({
  scanlineOpacity: 0.1 + (1 - sanity) * 0.3,
  chromaticOffset: 2 + (1 - sanity) * 5,
  glitchFrequency: Math.max(0, (0.7 - sanity) * 3),   // kicks in below 70%
  vignetteRadius: 0.9 - (1 - sanity) * 0.3,
});
```

This creates a visceral feedback loop. Losing Sanity makes the game harder to play, which makes you lose more Sanity. It mirrors the spiral of actual mental health crises — distress compounds, each layer making the next harder to manage. But here's the hopeful part: your Ghost Protocols from previous loops are still fighting. Your past self's defenses still hold. Progress persists even as things get harder.

### Sanity Recovery

Sanity can be partially restored through two mechanisms:

1. **Perfect wave clear:** If every enemy in a wave is killed before reaching the core, Sanity recovers by 3%.
2. **Neuroplasticity spending:** Between loops, players can spend Neuroplasticity to restore Sanity (5 NP per 5% Sanity).

These recovery options are intentionally stingy. Sanity should feel precious and fragile, not something you can freely regenerate. Every point of Sanity is a resource you're spending by failing to stop enemies, and getting it back requires either flawless play or sacrificing upgrade potential.

## Neuroplasticity: The Meta-Progression

Neuroplasticity (NP) is the most unique resource in Vega. It's earned at the **end of each loop** based on performance, and it's spent during the **inter-loop transition** on permanent upgrades.

### Earning Neuroplasticity

```typescript
const calculateNP = (loopStats: LoopStats): number => {
  let np = 0;
  np += loopStats.enemiesKilled * 0.5;
  np += loopStats.perfectWaves * 10;
  np += loopStats.sanitySaved * 0.3;     // bonus for high remaining sanity
  np += loopStats.towersPlaced >= 3 ? 5 : 0;  // build diversity bonus
  return Math.floor(np);
};
```

NP rewards comprehensive play. You don't just earn it by killing — you earn it for perfect waves, for maintaining Sanity, for using a variety of tower types. This pushes players toward well-rounded strategies rather than one-trick approaches.

### Spending Neuroplasticity

Between loops, the player enters an **inter-loop upgrade screen** with several spending options:

- **Tower Mastery:** Permanently increase a tower type's base stats by 5% per level (costs 15 NP per level)
- **Neural Fortification:** Increase starting Sanity for the next loop (costs 10 NP per 5%)
- **Pattern Recognition:** Reveal enemy spawn patterns for the next loop (costs 20 NP)
- **Synaptic Surge:** All Ghost Protocol towers gain +10% fire rate for the next loop (costs 25 NP)
- **Sanity Recovery:** Restore 5% Sanity (costs 5 NP)

The tension here is between **immediate survival** (Sanity recovery, knowing spawn patterns) and **long-term power** (Tower Mastery, Synaptic Surge). Spending NP on Sanity recovery keeps you alive but leaves your towers weaker. Investing in Tower Mastery makes future loops easier but doesn't help if you're about to die.

### Why Neuroplasticity Is Named That Way

The name is deliberate. Neuroplasticity — the brain's ability to reorganize and form new connections — is the real-world mechanism behind learning and recovery. In Vega, it represents the meta-learning that happens between loops. You survived Loop 1. What did you learn? How will you adapt? NP is the mechanical expression of growth.

## The Trinity in Action

The three resources create a decision-making landscape that's far richer than any single currency could achieve. Consider this scenario:

*You're in Loop 2 at 55% Sanity. A wave of Depression Hulks is approaching a corridor that's poorly defended. You have 80 Focus — enough for one Lobotomizer. But your Loop 1 Ghost Synapse in that corridor is doing work, and an Echo placed nearby would boost it significantly.*

**Do you build the Lobotomizer** (directly countering the threat but spending all your Focus) **or the Echo** (boosting your existing ghosts, saving Focus for future flexibility, but risking Hulks leaking through)?

And both choices exist within the Neuroplasticity context: if you get a perfect clear on this wave, you earn bonus NP for the Loop 2-3 transition. Is the risky Echo play worth the potential NP payoff?

That's three resources creating a single, rich decision moment. The Focus cost, the Sanity stakes, and the NP reward all matter simultaneously. This is what multi-resource design enables.

## Lessons in Resource Design

Building this system taught me that good resource design is about **creating dilemmas, not calculations**. If the optimal choice is always mathematically obvious, the resources are decorative. If the optimal choice depends on context, risk tolerance, and future planning, the resources are alive.

Focus, Sanity, and Neuroplasticity each pull the player in a different direction. Focus says "spend now." Sanity says "survive now." Neuroplasticity says "invest for later." That three-way tension is what makes Vega's economy feel urgent and meaningful in every loop.
