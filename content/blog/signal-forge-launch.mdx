# Signal Forge Launches: A Roguelike Deckbuilder About Perfect Timing

We're thrilled to announce the launch of **Signal Forge**, a roguelike deckbuilder that combines the card-building depth of games like Slay the Spire with a unique rhythm-matching mechanic. The core loop is simple but challenging: build a deck of waveforms, match sequences during combat, and survive increasingly difficult encounters.

## Core Mechanic: Sequence Matching

The heart of Signal Forge is sequence matching. During combat, each turn you'll:

1. **Draw Cards** - Your hand refills from your deck
2. **Play Cards** - Each card costs energy and has a type (Pulse, Sine, Saw, Noise)
3. **Match the Pattern** - The enemy announces a target sequence. If your played cards form that exact sequence, you get a bonus burst of damage
4. **Manage Pressure** - Playing the same waveform type multiple times adds "Static" corruption that you must stabilize

This creates a fascinating tension: do you play the cards that match the target sequence for guaranteed damage, or do you optimize your hand for safety and consistency?

## Deck Building & Synergies

Signal Forge includes 40 unique cards across four waveform types:

- **Pulse (Red)**: High damage, straightforward cards. ("Pulse Strike" - 6 damage)
- **Sine (Blue)**: Defensive cards with shield generation. ("Sine Guard" - 7 shield)
- **Saw (Green)**: Tempo-focused cards that draw extra cards. ("Saw Rush" - 5 damage, draw 1)
- **Noise (Purple)**: Support and utility with risky effects. ("Noise Cancel" - removes Static)

Each run, you'll visit shops where you can add new cards to your deck, removing cards you no longer need. The key is identifying synergies: maybe you're building a "Tempo Spam" deck that draws tons of cards and plays cheap sequences multiple times per turn. Or perhaps a "Shield Wall" strategy built on Sine cards.

## The Enemy Gauntlet

Signal Forge features procedurally-generated enemies with distinct archetypes:

- **Disruptor Drone** - Adds Static to pressure your signal integrity
- **Static Leech** - Feeds off your corruption
- **Rhythm Overlord** - Demands increasingly complex patterns
- **The Void** - A final boss that shifts patterns mid-combat

Each enemy announces their intent before their turn, allowing skilled players to read and react to threats.

## Tempo & Static: The Risk/Reward Loop

Two unique systems create emergent gameplay:

**Tempo Meter**: Increases when you successfully match sequences. Higher tempo means the next sequence bonus gets stronger (but it decays naturally). Managing tempo creates a rhythm to combat.

**Static Pressure**: Accumulates when you play duplicate types. Too much Static and your cards malfunction. You can stabilize using special cards, but it costs deck slots.

## Why Roguelike Deckbuilders?

After playing games like Slay the Spire, Balatro, and Inscryption, we were obsessed with how deckbuilders create emergent stories. You start with weak starter decks but discover combos that feel brilliant once they click. Signal Forge adds a rhythm dimension: not only does your deck need to work, but you need to execute sequences *correctly*.

It's the tension between:
- **Perfect Information** (you know your deck)
- **Imperfect Execution** (can you play the right cards in the right order?)
- **Strategic Depth** (deck synergies matter)
