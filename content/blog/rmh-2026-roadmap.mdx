## Building on Momentum

2025 was the year we proved that RMH Studios could ship. 2026 is the year we prove we can scale. Not scale in the Silicon Valley "growth at all costs" sense — scale in the sense of building deeper, richer experiences while maintaining the quality and honesty our community expects.

This roadmap is ambitious. It's also honest. We'll flag where we're confident, where we're exploring, and where we might be wrong. Roadmaps that only contain certainties aren't roadmaps — they're press releases.

## Games: Expanding What Works

### RMHbox: 25+ Minigames by Year End

RMHbox is our flagship, and we're doubling down on it. The target is to reach **25 or more minigames** in the catalog by the end of 2026, up from 16+ today.

But quantity isn't the goal — variety is. We're specifically targeting gaps in our current lineup:

- **Collaborative games** where the whole room works together instead of competing. Our player feedback consistently asks for co-op experiences.
- **Creative games** where players draw, write, or build things. These generate the most memorable moments and the most shareable content.
- **Physical games** that use device sensors — accelerometer, gyroscope — on mobile devices, turning phones into motion controllers for party play.

We're also building a **community minigame submission pipeline**. Players with game ideas will be able to pitch concepts, and selected ideas will be developed with credit to the creator. This isn't just community engagement — it's a recognition that our players have great ideas we'd never think of.

### Signal Forge: Full Campaign

Signal Forge's core deckbuilding loop is solid. In 2026, we're wrapping it in a full campaign structure:

- **Three acts** with distinct environments, enemy types, and mechanical themes.
- **A meta-progression system** where completed runs unlock new cards, characters, and modifiers for future runs. The roguelike loop needs long-term incentive structures, and we're building them.
- **Weekly challenge runs** with fixed seeds and community leaderboards. This gives competitive players a reason to replay and optimizers a canvas to work on.

### Echoes of the Spire: The Expansion

Echoes of the Spire's narrative extraction format resonated with players in ways we didn't expect. The expansion will add:

- **Two new narrative arcs**, each with their own set of documents, audio logs, and environmental storytelling.
- **Branching investigation paths** that change based on which evidence you prioritize.
- **Community theories board** — an in-game space where players can post and vote on theories about the story, creating a collaborative investigation layer.

### New Game Announcements

We have two new games in active development. We're not ready to announce details, but we'll share this much:

- One is a **multiplayer strategy game** that explores asynchronous play — think turns that happen over hours, not seconds. It's designed for people who want a game that fits into their day rather than demanding their full attention.
- The other is a **creative sandbox** that leverages WebGPU for real-time 3D rendering in the browser. This is our most technically ambitious project to date, and it's the testbed for our next-generation graphics pipeline.

We'll announce both properly when they're ready to show, with real gameplay footage — no mock-ups, no target renders, no lies.

## Platform: The Connective Tissue

Individual games are products. A platform is an ecosystem. In 2026, we're building the infrastructure that connects everything.

### Accounts and Profiles

This is the biggest platform investment of the year. We're building an **RMH account system** that spans all our games and tools:

- A single identity across RMHbox, Signal Forge, RMHTube, RMH Type, RMH Study, and all future products.
- **Player profiles** showing game history, achievements, and community contributions.
- **Friend lists** that work across games — see what your friends are playing and join them with one click.

We've deliberately delayed building accounts because we wanted to get it right. Account systems are trust infrastructure. They handle personal data. They need to be secure, private, and transparent about what data we collect and why. We're not bolting on auth as an afterthought — we're building it as a first-class platform feature.

### Cross-Game Progression

With accounts in place, we can build **cross-game rewards**:

- Daily game streaks in RMHdle unlock cosmetics in RMHbox.
- Signal Forge achievements unlock profile badges visible across all games.
- RMH Type milestones (words per minute records, total words typed) contribute to a global player level.

The goal is to make every game feel like part of a larger universe without forcing players to engage with games they're not interested in. Opt-in connection, not mandatory grinding.

### Improved Multiplayer Infrastructure

Our WebSocket infrastructure works, but 2026 demands we make it excellent:

- **Regional server nodes** to reduce latency for players outside our current server region.
- **Spectator mode** for RMHbox games, letting players watch ongoing rounds before joining.
- **Room persistence** so that game rooms can survive brief disconnections and browser refreshes without losing state.

## Technology: Betting on the Future

### WebGPU Adoption

As we discussed in our recent WebGPU post, 2026 is the year we start building on WebGPU in production. Our plan:

- Migrate Three.js rendering to the `WebGPURenderer` for supported browsers, with automatic WebGL fallback.
- Use compute shaders for particle effects, physics, and procedural generation in our new 3D sandbox game.
- Build a performance comparison dashboard to share real-world WebGPU vs. WebGL metrics with the community.

### Progressive Web App Improvements

We're investing in PWA capabilities to blur the line between browser game and native app:

- **Install prompts** for players who want our games on their home screen.
- **Offline mode** for single-player experiences like Signal Forge (using service workers to cache game state).
- **Push notifications** for daily game reminders, friend invites, and event announcements (opt-in only, obviously).

### Performance Budget System

We're implementing a formal performance budget for every game: maximum initial bundle size, maximum time-to-interactive, minimum frame rate on target hardware. If a feature pushes a game over budget, the feature gets optimized or cut. No exceptions.

## Industry Trends Shaping Our Plan

Several industry developments influence our 2026 strategy:

**The browser as gaming platform is gaining legitimacy.** Netflix Games has been exploring browser-based game delivery. Google's web gaming investments continue. The conversation has shifted from "can the browser run games?" to "what kinds of games run best in the browser?"

**AI tools are accelerating development.** We're using AI-assisted development for code generation, asset creation, and testing. This doesn't replace human creativity and judgment — it amplifies our small team's output. We expect AI tooling to be a significant multiplier for indie studios in 2026.

**Community-driven development is the new normal.** Early access, public roadmaps, transparent communication — these aren't novel anymore. Players expect to be partners in development, not just customers. Our community-first approach, which felt unusual when we started, is becoming industry standard.

**WebGPU reaches critical mass.** With Chrome and Safari supporting WebGPU and Firefox closing the gap, 2026 is the year browser games can realistically target modern GPU features without alienating large portions of the audience.

## What We're Not Doing

Honest roadmaps include what you've decided *against*:

- **We're not building a native app.** The browser is our platform. Wrapping our games in Electron or shipping to app stores would compromise our core thesis.
- **We're not raising venture capital.** VC money comes with growth expectations that conflict with building patiently and honestly. We're bootstrapping.
- **We're not chasing trends.** We won't build a battle royale because they're popular, or add blockchain because it's buzzy. We build what serves our players and our creative vision.

## The Honesty Clause

Some items on this roadmap won't ship in 2026. That's the nature of ambitious plans executed by a small team. When things slip or change, we'll tell you why. When we cut something, we'll explain the reasoning. When we're wrong about a bet, we'll own it.

A roadmap is a promise to try, not a guarantee of delivery. We take that promise seriously.

2026 is going to be big. Let's build it together.
